## PREMISE

Every piece of software on earth is about to be rewritten in the next 24
months. The only question is whether what gets built on the other side is
authentic human craft or more algorithmic landfill. This is a spec-ops manual
for smuggling meaningful products past corporate gatekeepers, a guerrilla
playbook for making "good trouble" inside the machine, and a warning label for
the soul-cost of building the wrong thing. It carries a serialized cyberpunk
graphic novel — "The Broadcast" — through every chapter, following ZERO, a
burned-out engineer who quit a dating app that weaponized loneliness and now
fights to build something real inside a city run by a corporate OS called PRISM.

## THE FIVE CLAIMS (the thesis — every chapter reinforces one or more)

1. Every line of software on earth is about to be rewritten.
2. Authenticity is the real currency of the future.
3. The enterprise is a physics problem, not a sales problem.
4. The corporate immune system can be bypassed, but not defeated.
5. Good trouble is the only kind of trouble worth making.

## THREE PILLARS

1. THE PHYSICS — equations that model corporate inertia, fear, entropy, and
   bureaucratic friction as literal forces of nature (24 total; see canon.md).
2. THE GRAPHIC NOVEL — "The Broadcast," a cyberpunk narrative running through
   every chapter as cold-open vignettes.
3. THE APP — a companion app (Space Tokens economy) that listens to meetings
   and computes the formulas live (Appendix C).

## THE DORIAN GRAY METAPHOR (central)

The PRODUCT stays beautiful (clean UI, high NPS, awards) while the SERVER ROOM
portrait rots (manipulation algorithms, addiction loops, exploitation metrics).
The prettier the interface, the uglier the engine must be to hold growth
targets. The beauty is a prerequisite for the exploitation. Runs from
MirrorMatch in the Prologue to the "portrait healing" in Chapter 16.

## CHAPTER FORMAT TEMPLATE (every chapter follows this — do not deviate)

1. CHAPTER HEADER — number (bold), title, italic subtitle
2. GLOSSARY OF TERMS — 8–12 terms, **Term** — definition, military-intel tone
3. SITREP — Situation / Complication / Main Point, compressed urgent prose
4. THE BROADCAST — graphic-novel vignette, cinematic present tense, advances
   ZERO's arc, 2–5 described panels, characters act per the bible
5. BRIEFING — 3–7 numbered Intel Blocks; equations get name, formula, variable
   defs, traffic-light benchmarks, 2–3 worked field-report examples
6. BOSS PROFILE — the chapter's gatekeeper: Intel / Strategy / The Pitch
7. KEY MISSION TASKS — numbered, specific, measurable, military-order voice
8. GLASS HOUSE EXERCISES — 3–4 named practical exercises with real data
9. SPZ VOICE CLOSER — first-person confessional, raw, 200–400 words
10. CHAPTER END MARKER — "End of Chapter X — Next: [title]"

Field reports (Ch 13–15) may compress the template into case-study form.

## BENCHMARK SYSTEM (all equations)
