# CHAPTER 10: THE QUALIFICATION PROTOCOL
## *Avoiding Zombies*

> STATUS: SCAFFOLD (+ v0 draft available for import). Target ~3,400 words.

## SITREP
- Situation: your calendar is full; meetings, demos, POCs. You feel momentum.
- Complication: 80% of your pipeline is dead and too polite to say so.
- Main Point: your most valuable resource is time. Kill the dead deals ruthlessly.

## THE BROADCAST
Tucker, 9 PM. An inbound from "Derek Huang, Director of Innovation, FutureCorp" —
"explore the art of the possible," "great synergy," "socialize internally." Zombie
Score: 0. ZERO sends the Breakup Email. Three days later: Lisa Yamamoto, VP of
Compliance Ops — 8,000 SARs/quarter, missing the deadline by 6 days, consent-order
threat, $200K approved for Q2. Score: 59. ZERO replies one line: "Thursday at 10
AM work for you?" The zombie was the gatekeeper, not the company.

## EQUATIONS
- Score = (Pain_Owner × 5) + (Timeline × 3) + (Budget_Code × 5). < 10 → kill it.
- I_v (Vaporware Index) = Adjectives_in_Pitch / Live_Demo_Minutes. Works both ways;
  if YOUR I_v > 10, you're the vaporware.

## KEY CONCEPTS
Zombie archetypes (Vanity Visionary, Innovation Tourist, Poisoned Champion); the
Zombie Lexicon; the Three Questions ("Who gets fired if this fails?" "Show me the
budget code." "Can we deploy in 30 days?"); the Breakup Email (loss aversion).

## BOSS
The Innovation Lead — don't pitch them, USE them: "Who actually owns this pain?
Can you introduce us?"

## INFLUENCES TO DEPLOY
Diogenes (the lamp — looking for one honest decision-maker); Thompson (the breakup
email as a gonzo nuclear option).

## SPZ CLOSER
Six months nurturing a Fortune 100 corpse; the Breakup Email on Tuesday; the real
VP calling Thursday. "Kill the zombies. The living are waiting behind them."
