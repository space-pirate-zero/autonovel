# CHAPTER 8: GOOD TROUBLE
## *How to Be John Lewis Inside a Fortune 500*

> STATUS: SCAFFOLD (+ v0 draft available for import). Target ~3,600 words.
> Reinforces Claim 5. PLANT F3: BISHOP's crooked badge (payoff Ch 12).

## SITREP
- Situation: some insurgents are already inside — with badges, 401(k)s, and
  knowledge of every back door.
- Complication: the internal insurgent risks career destruction; every act of
  rebellion bets against their own security.
- Main Point: good trouble is precision, not recklessness — know the policies
  better than their enforcers; build a case so airtight that punishing you is
  more embarrassing than celebrating you.

## THE BROADCAST
BISHOP's origin, three years ago: Keisha Williams (24) fired for a $2M-saving tool
built without ARB approval — the VP who championed a $3M vendor filed the HR
complaint. Bishop watches through glass, badge still straight. That night he reads
247 pages of procurement policy + 189 of architecture standards, finds 23
loopholes. Over three years he uses every one: "training" classification,
"enhancement not system," L8 read-only access. 12 tools, $14M saved, zero ARB
reviews. His badge now hangs 15–20° crooked. Nobody knows why.

## EQUATIONS
- GT (Good Trouble Coefficient) = (Impact_change × Alignment_values) /
  (Risk_career + Friction_bureaucracy). If Alignment_values = 0, GT collapses.
- I_insurgent = Problems_Solved_quietly / Credit_Claimed_publicly → ∞ = max power.
