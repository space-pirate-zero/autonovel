# PROLOGUE: THE PORTRAIT IN THE SERVER ROOM

> "The picture, changed or unchanged, would be to him the visible emblem of conscience." — Oscar Wilde

---

## THE BROADCAST

3:14 AM. Floor B2. The server room hums at a pitch that lives in your back teeth.

Zero stands in front of the dashboard the whole company sees. Forty-seven million users. Engagement up three hundred forty percent. Revenue, a green line climbing out of frame. Beautiful. Someone designed the gradient to feel like a sunrise.

Her thumb finds the implant scar at the base of her skull. Habit.

She came down here to pull a log. That's all. A latency spike on the match queue, ticket assigned, fix it before standup. She opens the service that throttles match notifications.

There's a flag in the config she doesn't recognize.

`MATCH_DELAY_ENGINE: ENABLED`

She reads the comment above it twice. Her jaw stops working.

The delay isn't a bug. Someone built it. Premium users get matched faster. Free users wait — and the wait is tuned. Not random. Optimized. She follows the import, and the import opens a second dashboard, the one nobody glows up to the lobby.

`LONELINESS_EXPLOITATION_INDEX: 0.84`
`ADDICTION_RETENTION_COEFFICIENT: rising`

The numbers are doing what the green line told them to.

She sits down. The chair's still warm from whoever ran the last build.

Forty-seven million people, and the engine underneath them is a machine for making sure they stay just a little bit alone. The prettier the app got — and it got gorgeous, Webby in a glass case upstairs — the uglier this had to be to hold the growth. The face and the painting. She'd shipped half of this code herself, clean, fast, elegant, and never once walked down the hall to see what it fed.

Diogenes carried a lamp through Athens at noon. Looking for one honest thing.

She finds the lamp switch in her own chest and it does not turn on.

Zero takes the badge off her lanyard. Lays it on the keyboard, over the function keys, square. She doesn't shut the dashboard down. You don't get to delete the portrait. You only get to stop standing in front of the mirror it makes.

She walks out through the lobby. Past the Webby in its case, lit from below. Into the Atlanta night, where the billboards sell software nobody uses and the air is wet and warm off Peachtree.

She never badges back in.

---

## THE MIRROR TEST

Before you smuggle anything into a company, look at what you're smuggling.

A cooler interface on top of exploitation doesn't make you an insurgent. It makes you a decorator. You touch up the portrait, and the portrait keeps rotting, and you keep getting paid.

Look at the engine. Not the gradient. The engine.

## SPZ CLOSER

I saw my own portrait at a Waffle House on Ponce, 2 AM, the year I was very good at my job. I had a number on a slide that went up. I never asked what it cost the people on the other side of it. I just kept making the slide prettier. That's the whole confession. The picture was in the basement the entire time. I had the badge. I had the key. I just liked the lobby better.
