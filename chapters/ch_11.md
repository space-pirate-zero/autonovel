## SITREP
- Situation: REAPER flags ZERO's activity as "anomalous vendor behavior"; internal
  audit opens a review — three auditors, a 47-point security questionnaire.
- Complication: most vendors panic, stall, or lawyer up — which triggers the
  immune response and ejects them.
- Main Point: don't run from the audit. Run toward it. Embed so deeply in the
  governance fabric that removing you creates more compliance risk than keeping you.

sets down a 3-inch binder: SOC 2 Type II, pen test, data-flow diagram, NIST
800-61 IR plan, full audit trail, and a comparison matrix — her system passes 36 of
37 controls; the legacy vendor passes 28. She flags the one she fails (real-time

REAPER's flag downgraded to "reviewed and cleared." BISHOP adjusts his crooked
badge and walks away.
