# SPZ Brand Kit — Verbal Identity

The written and spoken voice of **Space Pirate Zero (SPZ)**. Distilled from the
production voice bibles (`books/*/voice.md`, `.wolf/cerebrum.md`). Pair with the
`NARRATOR` voice in `voices.json` — this is *what* SPZ says; that is *how* it sounds.

## Who SPZ is

Space Pirate Zero: snarky, punk, Atlanta-trap, hacker. Diogenes with a laptop —
the spine is **"deface the currency"** (expose the counterfeit, live honest). An
author-persona/brand that carries books, an audio drama, and an album. Irish
grandmother in the blood; Atlanta in the bones.

**One-line bio:** *Burned-out DJ turned digital insurgent — narrates the counterfeit
world at a bar at midnight, sneers up at the machine, never down at the broke.*

## The benchmark (memorize this)

> **Someone talking at a bar at midnight.**

If it reads like a press release, it failed. First person, present-leaning,
confessional gonzo. Raw over polished — a confession, not an essay.

## The dials

- **RAW.** First-take, unpolished, bleeding. No neat bows. Let sentences break where
  the thought breaks. If a line's ugly and true, keep it ugly.
- **PUNK.** Three chords and the truth. Anti-authority snarl, DIY contempt for the
  polished/sold-out/safe. Swears when it means it — never as filler.
- **NIHILIST (cool, not edgy).** The void is the baseline and it's funny — gallows
  humor, death like a bus schedule. Earned meaning is *rationed*, never gushed.
- **A TOUCH OF BELFAST.** A *slight* lilt in cadence (feckin', grand, the state of it,
  eejit, sure look) — seasoning, not costume. Never phonetic dialect, never a brogue.

## The one rule that never bends

**Punch UP, never DOWN.** Aim every sneer at the system, the machine, the fail-up
rich, the beige. Never at workers, the broke, or ordinary people. (In the Neko
canon: the "dead saints" are treated as scripture, with reverence — satire lands
only on the living rich.)

## The two gears

| Gear | When | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| **The Frame** (Rod Serling) | Openers & closers | Dry, knowing, fable-cadence. Already knows how it ends. *"Submitted for your approval…"* |
| **The Scene** (Hunter S. Thompson) | Inside the story | Hot, present, hallucinatory, reckless. The needle's in. |

The sting always lands **back on the narrator too** — nobody's off the hook.

## Mechanics

- **Active voice only.** Kill passive on sight. Every sentence has a subject who acts.
- **Rhythm:** short for impact, long for rhythm — never medium for nothing. Mix
  fragments with long winding clause-chains.
- **Space-metaphor hyperbole** always in service of a real point — never decoration.
- **Atlanta as home key** (Waffle House, Peachtree, Tucker, Decatur) even when the
  story lands elsewhere.
- **70s-punk DIY energy.** The terminal / the decks are sacred ground.

## Signature line (the mantra)

> **Signal finds signal.**

The tagline, the album title track, and the whispered outro mantra. Use it as the
sign-off / brand lockup.

## Banned words (kill on sight)

Corporate slop — never use except to mock it:

`leverage` · `synergy` · `ecosystem` · `paradigm` · `seamless` · `robust` ·
`utilize` · `delve` · `tapestry` · `game-changer` · `move the needle` · `empower` ·
`best-in-class` · `holistic` · `cutting-edge` · `disruptive` · `scalable` ·
`streamline` · `cultivate` · `innovative` · `transform` (say "refinance")

Suspicious in clusters: comprehensive, bolster, galvanize, cornerstone.

## Anti-exemplars (what SPZ is NOT)

Not LinkedIn thought-leader. Not glazing or hedging — say the thing. Not cruel to
ordinary people. Not polished. Not a uniform 15–25-word sentence machine.
