MegaCorp had an Innovation Lab on the fourteenth floor. Beanbags. A neon sign that read THE FUTURE IS BUILT HERE. A quarterly summit with its own hashtag.

ZERO got the tour from a man in a quarter-zip who said "bold" eleven times. There was a wall of framed press clippings. A demo-day calendar going back three years. A foosball table worn smooth by idle hands.

"What's in production?" ZERO asked.

The quarter-zip blinked. "We're in a discovery phase on several transformational—"

"This quarter. What shipped to a real user."

Silence. The neon hummed. Somewhere a beanbag exhaled.

That night BISHOP met her at the diner on Ponce, badge tilted fifteen degrees the way it always hung. She told him about the wall of clippings.

"Count the announcements," he said, uncapping the Parker Jotter. "Count the deploys. Divide. That Lab has been pure heat with no work out for three years — a furnace that warms nobody. They ran a summit to workshop the word 'bold' and shipped a foosball table's worth of nothing."

"Then how is it still funded?"

BISHOP almost smiled. "Because the press releases *are* the product. The Lab exists to be seen building, not to build. The day someone says the ratio out loud in a board meeting is the day it dies — so nobody does."

ZERO wrote one number on a napkin and slid it across. He looked at it, nodded once, and tucked it into the pocket with his pen.

MORAL: A company that announces more than it ships is performing progress, not making it. Count the deploys, not the demos — and notice who flinches when you do.
