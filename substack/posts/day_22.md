# Day 22/30 — The Zombie Scorecard
*A fable from the field manual · Chapter 10*

The email arrived at 4:47 p.m.: an "Innovation Lead" at FutureCorp wanted to "explore the art of the possible" around a "transformational partnership" that would "leverage synergies across the innovation portfolio."

ZERO read it twice and counted. Not one verb anyone could file a ticket against. No problem. No system. No person bleeding. Just a *culture of disruption* — the sound a building makes when it has nothing to say.

She ran the scorecard out loud while GHOST watched. Pain — does this person own a problem he can name? No; he owns a portfolio of exploration. Zero. Timeline — a by-when with a consequence? Never. Zero. Budget — does he control money? His title was *Office of the Future.* The future doesn't carry a P-card. Zero.

Total: zero. A corpse with good color.

"Color isn't a pulse," ZERO said. "It's the first thing a corpse keeps."

She didn't pitch. She sent four sentences — a breakup email, the most honest version of Diogenes walking Athens with his lamp: *I don't think the timing's right; I'll close our file; reach out if the pain ever gets real.* Then she let the dead deal lie there, green in the pipeline, cold on the slab.

Three days later her phone rang. Not the Innovation Lead — the actual VP of Compliance: eight thousand reports a quarter, a regulator's deadline, two hundred thousand dollars already approved. ZERO ran the number again. Fifty-nine. A pulse.

The zombie was never the company. It was the gatekeeper. Walk away from the corpse and the living come find you.

---

**What it means.** Most insurgents don't die failing to sell — they die succeeding in selling to a corpse. Three variables, five minutes. Under 10, kill the deal. The friendly 'Innovation Lead' with no budget and no deadline is the walking dead of your pipeline.

## The equation

$$ Score = (Pain\_Owner \times 5) + (Timeline \times 3) + (Budget\_Code \times 5) $$

🟢 45–65 (live) · 🟡 25–44 (one vital missing) · 🔴 0–24 (corpse — send the breakup email).

**The moral of the story is:** Most deals die not from failing to sell but from selling to a corpse. Take the pulse — pain, timeline, budget — before you spend your life on it. The living call back; the dead stay dead.
