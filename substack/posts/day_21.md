# Day 21/30 — The Self-Funding Coefficient
*Chapter 9*

## The nugget
If this is over 5, the pilot pays for itself five times over and the CFO is negligent not to approve it. This isn't a cost — it's a reallocation from a wasteful line item to a productive one. Below 5? Go back to the User Buyer and expand the use case until it clears.

## The equation

$$ S_{fc} = \frac{Cost_{human} - Cost_{AI}}{Cost_{pilot}} $$

🟢 > 5 (pays for itself) · 🟢🟢 > 10 (free money) · 🔴 < 1 (don't pitch the CFO).

## SPZ closer

> Richard Branson started Virgin with a student magazine and a record shop, not a war chest. The scrappy-empire move is always the same: find the trade so lopsided that saying no looks irrational. Self-funding is that trade. You're not asking the suit to believe in your vision — you're asking him to stop setting money on fire. Frame it so the pilot is cheaper than the status quo before the first invoice clears, and the question stops being 'should we?' and becomes 'why didn't we yesterday?'
