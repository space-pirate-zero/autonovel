# Day 9/30 — Consensus Paralysis
*A fable from the field manual · Chapter 2*

MegaCorp formed a committee to decide whether to approve ZERO's tool. Twelve members — because every department demanded a seat, and a seat meant a veto.

The first meeting produced a follow-up meeting. The second produced an action item to schedule a workshop. By the fourth, two members had been swapped for people who needed the whole thing re-explained, and the original problem — a team drowning in reports — had not moved one inch closer to a fix.

ZERO did the arithmetic for BISHOP afterward. Twelve people. Sixty-six separate lines of tension between them, each a fresh way to say no, and not one new way to say yes. The room was a graveyard with a catering budget.

"You will never get a yes from that table," BISHOP said. "Every head you add multiplies the paths to no and adds none to yes. They didn't build a committee to decide. They built it so no single person has to."

"So what do I do?"

"Shrink the deciders. Widen the beneficiaries." He capped the pen. "Find the one person who owns the pain and can sign for it. Help everyone else without asking permission. A movement isn't everyone in the room agreeing — it's a small core acting while a wide base quietly benefits."

ZERO walked out of the committee that afternoon and never went back. She found Sarah — who owned the pain and could approve a tool under the signing threshold — and by Friday it was running on nine desks.

The committee, she heard later, was still meeting.

---

**What it means.** Metcalfe's Law of friction. A 10-person meeting has 45 lines of tension. Every head you add multiplies paths to 'no' and adds none to 'yes.' Committees are graveyards. Keep the room small enough to fit a decision.

## The equation

$$ F_{cp} = \frac{n(n-1)}{2} $$

🟢 ≤ 6 (n≤4) · 🟡 10–21 (n 5–7) · 🔴 > 28 (n ≥ 8).

**The moral of the story is:** Every person you add to a decision multiplies the ways to say no and adds none to say yes. Shrink the room to the one who owns the pain; let the rest simply benefit.
