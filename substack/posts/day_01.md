# Day 1/30 — The Rewrite
*Chapter 1 — the manifesto*

## The nugget
In the next 24 months AI will rewrite more software than humans wrote in the last 24 years. Not incrementally — categorically. The doom-criers miss the twist: AI eats *generic* software and makes *authentic* software more valuable than ever. The scarce thing was never code. It's taste — deciding what *should* exist, not what *can*. This series is 30 days of the physics that decides which side of that line your work lands on.

## SPZ closer

> Diogenes the Cynic lived in a barrel and walked Athens at noon with a lit lamp, hunting one honest man. His actual oracle was two words: *deface the currency.* He stamped over the king's face on real coins to prove it was a story people agreed to believe. That's the whole job now. The corporate currency is bullshit — synergy, leverage, transformation. The machines mint infinite bullshit faster than you can. So stop competing on it. Stamp over it. Smuggle something real inside the counterfeit and hand it back. The machines are coming for the boring parts. Good. We're here for the parts that need a pulse.
