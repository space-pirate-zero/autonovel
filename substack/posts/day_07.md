# Day 7/30 — Legacy Friction
*A fable from the field manual · Chapter 2*

There was a box in MegaCorp's basement rack that everyone called The Oracle, though no one remembered why, and no one alive had opened it.

It had been running since before the cloud migration. Its documentation was four pages, two of them blank. Every system in the building leaned on it, and every engineer routed carefully around it — the way villagers walk wide of a sleeping animal.

When ZERO asked, three different teams said the same sentence: "Don't touch The Oracle." The friction wasn't technical. It was fear — a system so old and so undocumented that touching it felt like defusing a bomb in the dark.

BISHOP found her staring at the rack diagram. "High friction," he said. "Years since anyone updated it, over the pages of docs that actually explain it. The number's enormous. Everyone reads that as *danger*."

"And you read it as—"

"An opening." He clicked his pen. "Fear is just understanding nobody bothered to acquire. The day you become the one person who actually knows what The Oracle does, you stop being a vendor. You become load-bearing. They can't cut what they don't understand — and you will."

So ZERO did the thing no one would do. She read it. All of it. Traced every call, mapped every undocumented quirk, wrote the fifth page, and the sixth.

By winter, when The Oracle finally hiccuped, there was exactly one person in the building who could calm it. The fortress had a heartbeat now, and it ran through her.

---

**What it means.** How hard a system is to change, measured by its obscurity and the fear it generates. High friction — old and undocumented — is terrifying to everyone, which means it's your opening. Become the only person who actually understands it and you become load-bearing.

## The equation

$$ \mu_{leg} = \frac{Years\_Since\_Update}{Pages\_of\_Documentation} $$

🟢 low · 🟡 mid · 🔴 high (obscure + terrifying; nobody will touch it).

**The moral of the story is:** The scary, undocumented system everyone routes around is your way in. Read what no one else will; become the one person the fortress can't run without.
