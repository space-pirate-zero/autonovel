# Day 19/30 — The Insurgent Index
*Chapter 8*

## The nugget
The best internal rebels fix everything and claim nothing. They give the credit away, build a network of quiet debtors, and become load-bearing in ways nobody can quite articulate — which is exactly why they can't be cut. Power through competence, not visibility.

## The equation

$$ I_{insurgent} = \frac{Problems\_Solved_{quietly}}{Credit\_Claimed_{publicly}} $$

🟢 → ∞ (max power) · 🔴 → 0 (you're building a LinkedIn, not a movement).

## SPZ closer

> Bernie again: not me, us — and he meant it enough to make 'boring' a superpower. The most dangerous person in any building is the one with nothing on their LinkedIn and everything running through their hands. Give your manager the win. Let the VP present 'their' initiative. Each person who takes credit for your work now owes you a favor they can never quite repay, and quietly, without a single announcement, you become the gravity. Visibility is a tax on the insecure. Pay it only when it buys something.
