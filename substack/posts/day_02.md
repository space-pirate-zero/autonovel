# Day 2/30 — The Extinction Rate
*A fable from the field manual · Chapter 1*

ZERO's apartment in Tucker, 2 a.m., three monitors throwing pink and cyan across the dark. She'd pulled the public code of nine dead companies out of the Graveyard and fed it to GHOST.

"Run it," she said.

The numbers came back in cyan. SCALEWELL: ninety-one percent test coverage, a design system three other startups had copied, a ratio that read like a death certificate. Almost every line it ever shipped was a line the machine could now throw a thousand times before breakfast.

*Watch,* GHOST said. On the center monitor a model began rebuilding SCALEWELL's flagship from nothing — auth, dashboard, billing glue — and finished in under a minute. Correct. Clean. Indistinguishable from eighteen months of a team's life.

"And this one?" ZERO pulled up a narrow logistics tool nobody had heard of, built by one woman who understood freight law in three countries the way a sommelier knows a hillside.

The model tried. It produced something that compiled and *lied* — confident, plausible, wrong in the exact places where the law had teeth. Her tool's ratio sat near zero. The machine could copy the shape. It could not copy the scar tissue.

"So the generic is already dead," ZERO said.

*It just hasn't been told yet,* GHOST said. *The velocity only climbs. The denominator is the only thing you hold.*

She opened a terminal and started a list. Not features — decisions. The handful of things in her own work that had required a human to be there, awake, with taste and a stake. The list was short.

That was the point.

---

**What it means.** How fast a product gets regenerated out of existence. As AI velocity climbs, only the code carrying genuine human judgment survives. A CRUD form with a gradient has a near-infinite numerator — it's landfill the moment the tooling catches up. The defensible work is the weird work: the parts a model can't infer because they required a decision only you would make.

## The equation

$$ R_{ext} = \frac{Lines_{generic}}{Lines_{authentic}} \times AI_{velocity} $$

🟢 < 1 · 🟡 1–5 · 🔴 > 5 (you're landfill). Worked: a generic dashboard, ratio ~10, velocity rising → 🔴, measured in months.

**The moral of the story is:** Multiply how copyable your work is by how fast the machines improve, and you get the date it vanishes. The only thing that moves that date is the judgment only you could have made.
