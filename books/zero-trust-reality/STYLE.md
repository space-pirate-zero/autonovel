# Voice & Style — *The Last Human CEO* (rewrite spec)

The book is a **tragic dark comedy**. POV: close third, single, on Cope.
Narration is framed by the **Space Pirate Zero "transmissions"** (one per
chapter opener; Rod-Serling-cum-pirate-radio). Keep the satire and the
jokes. But the rewrite fuses three engines:

## The three engines (blend them; don't alternate in clean blocks)

1. **Hunter S. Thompson — the body and the velocity.** Gonzo, visceral,
   first-thought-fast sensory overload, especially for the cocaine, the
   mania, the rooms, the nights. Hyperbole that's *accurate*. Lists that
   accelerate. The reek and glare and chemical weather of a scene. Drugs
   rendered with merciless specificity, never glamour. Comedy lives here —
   the manic grandiosity, the absurd luxury, the bit that goes too far.
   *Calibration:* "He had not slept since Tuesday and it was a Friday that
   felt personally engineered to humiliate him, the light in the Aerie too
   bright and too clean, every surface reporting his pulse back to him, and
   somewhere in his sinuses a small bright wire of last night's certainty
   still singing the national anthem of himself."

2. **Victor Hugo — the moral altitude and the sweep.** Pull back from the
   man to the age: the firing of the last humans, the arithmetic of
   worth, the town with the dead in it. The purposeful digression that
   indicts a system. Compassion even for the wretched; the narrator who
   judges the world, not just the man. Grandeur without sentimentality.
   *Calibration:* "There is a kind of arithmetic a civilization does only
   once, quietly, in the language of severance and share price, and calls
   progress; and the men it subtracts are never told the sum, only handed
   a crystal obelisk and asked to smile while the room counts down."

3. **Dostoevsky — the interior and the guilt.** Feverish consciousness,
   self-justification collapsing into confession, the spiritual stakes of
   degradation, the man arguing with himself and losing, the buried sin
   that surfaces. Anguish that is *psychological*, not decorative. The
   reader inside the rationalization as it rots.
   *Calibration:* "He had decided in eleven minutes and spent six years
   proving to himself that eleven minutes was not a decision but a
   scheduling conflict, and the proof had held, the proof had always
   held, right up until a machine that could not be lied to read the
   eleven minutes back to him in his own company's voice and he understood
   that he had known, that he had always known, that knowing was the whole
   of what he was and the one thing he could not survive."

## The Space Pirate Zero transmissions (chapter openers)

Keep the **Twilight Zone / Rod Serling** key — the measured, ironic,
morally-loaded setup ("Picture, if you will…", "Submitted for your
consideration…"). But the narrator is now explicitly a **storyteller
relaying a tale of woe directly to the reader/listener.** He is a pirate
broadcaster on a salvage ship (Spaceship Alpha 9) who has picked up the
signal of this doomed man's life and is telling it to *you*, the only one
still tuned in — intimate, conspiratorial, second-person ("pull your chair
close, friend," "lean in," "stay with me"), aware it ends badly and
telling it anyway because someone should. Each transmission:
- addresses the reader and frames the chapter as the next movement of the
  tale he is relaying;
- keeps Serling's irony and moral altitude (this is a *cautionary* tale);
- ends on a forward hook ("next stop…", "stay with me", "watch what he
  does now");
- signs off: *— Space Pirate Zero, transmitting from Spaceship Alpha 9.*
Tonally it should feel like a man telling you, by firelight on a dead ship,
the saddest true story he knows, and not flinching from the end.

## Rules

- **Funny AND devastating in the same paragraph.** The comedy makes the
  tragedy land; never one without the other for long.
- **The drugs cost.** Visceral, escalating, never cool. Bodies fail.
- **Restraint on the worst things.** The Hearthstone deaths and the
  suicide get GRAVITY, not gore. The suicide act is never diagrammed —
  cut to black. Thompson's velocity is for the mania, not the death.
- **The machine never gets interiority.** Darlene is quoted only via
  rationales/letters/logs — flat, clean, accurate, wanting nothing. The
  horror is the contrast between its flat voice and the fevered human one.
- **Hold the ambiguity.** The hazing and the mania must stay
  unresolvable. Never let the narrator confirm whether Darlene is cruel.
- **Sentences earn their length.** Long Faulknerian/Hugo runs are allowed
  and wanted, but they must accelerate or deepen, never just sprawl.
- **Keep continuity to canon.md.** Atlanta; Darlene; Theo=Darlene; the
  cocaine curve; the ending mechanics.
