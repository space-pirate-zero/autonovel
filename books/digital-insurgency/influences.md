# Influences — the Real Figures Threaded Through Digital Insurgency

Use these HEAVILY — as epigraphs, Broadcast texture, briefing analogies, and
SPZ-closer anchors. Rules: quote honestly (real words attributed; stories
paraphrased and flagged as such); never decoration; punch up, never down. The
"⚠ cautionary" figures are mirrors for the SYSTEM (PRISM/REAPER/the Council),
never heroes — handle with care and without glorification.

The spine: **Diogenes' mandate — "deface the currency."** The corporate currency
is bullshit (synergy, leverage, transformation). The insurgent counterfeits it,
smuggles real value inside the counterfeit, and hands it back. Every figure below
is a facet of that one move.

---

## Oscar Wilde — the central metaphor
- WHY: *The Picture of Dorian Gray* IS the book's engine. The product stays
  beautiful; the portrait in the server room rots.
- QUOTES: "Every portrait that is painted with feeling is a portrait of the
  artist, not of the sitter." / "The books that the world calls immoral are books
  that show the world its own shame."
- DEPLOY: Prologue + Ch 5 + Ch 16. The prettier the UI, the uglier the engine.

## Diogenes the Cynic — the patron saint (note: Greek, not Roman)
- WHY: Homeless philosopher in a barrel who told Alexander the Great, the most
  powerful man alive, to move out of his sunlight. Walked Athens with a lamp in
  daylight "looking for an honest man." His oracle: **deface the currency.**
- QUOTES: "Stand a little out of my sun." / "I am looking for an honest man."
- DEPLOY: ZERO vs. the Council (the barrel vs. the throne). The lamp = hunting the
  fingerprint (Ch 3). Defacing the currency = the whole thesis.

## Miyamoto Musashi — the discipline / the Oath
- WHY: The *Dokkōdō* ("The Way of Walking Alone"), 21 precepts written days before
  death. *The Book of Five Rings*.
- QUOTES: "Accept everything just the way it is." / "Do nothing that is of no use."
- DEPLOY: The Insurgent's Oath (Ch 16) echoes the Dokkōdō. The insurgent walks
  alone but is not lonely. Economy of motion = ZERO's boxing.

## Che Guevara — the awakening + guerrilla doctrine
- WHY: *The Motorcycle Diaries* (the journey that radicalized him by making him
  SEE suffering) maps onto ZERO's portrait moment. *Guerrilla Warfare* = asymmetric
  tactics (the wedge, the sidecar). UN 1964 speech = speaking truth in the enemy's
  own chamber (ZERO before the Council).
- QUOTES: "The true revolutionary is guided by great feelings of love." / "Let me
  say, at the risk of seeming ridiculous, that the true revolutionary is guided by
  great feelings of love."
- DEPLOY: Ch 1/5 (the awakening); Ch 7/12 (asymmetric strikes). The wedge is foco.

## Hunter S. Thompson — the SPZ voice register
- WHY: Gonzo journalism; "my attorney" / Dr. Gonzo (Oscar Zeta Acosta) as the
  unreliable co-conspirator. The SPZ closers run hottest here.
- QUOTES: "Buy the ticket, take the ride." / "We can't stop here, this is bat
  country." / "When the going gets weird, the weird turn pro."
- DEPLOY: SPZ closers; GHOST can play a cooler "my attorney" foil. "Only the weird
  survives" rhymes with Thompson.

## Michael Collins — the intelligence war
- WHY: Beat an empire with information, not numbers — the Twelve Apostles, breaking
  the British spy network. Asymmetry through knowing the system better than it
  knows itself.
- QUOTES: "We struck at the eyes and ears." (paraphrase of his intelligence
  doctrine)
- DEPLOY: BISHOP as the inside intelligence man (Ch 8/11). Reading the policy
  better than its authors.

## Fred Hampton — the coalition + the cost
- WHY: The Rainbow Coalition — organizing across factions. Murdered in his bed by
  the state at 21. The price of being effective.
- QUOTES: "You can kill a revolutionary, but you can't kill the revolution." / "I
  am a revolutionary."
- DEPLOY: Coalition inside the fortress (Sarah + Bishop + the teams). The honest
  stakes — REAPER doesn't kill, it adjusts your credit score (the bureaucratic
  version of the same erasure).

## Malcolm X — self-education + transformation
- WHY: Taught himself by reading the dictionary in prison. Capable of total
  reinvention (Nation → Mecca → El-Hajj Malik El-Shabazz).
- QUOTES: "By any means necessary." / "Education is the passport to the future."
- DEPLOY: BISHOP reading 247 pages of policy (Ch 8) = the prison-library move.
  ZERO's arc = principled reinvention.

## The Prophet Muhammad — the message and the migration (⚠ respectful)
- WHY: *Iqra* ("Read/Recite") in the cave of Hira; the Hijra to Medina as a
  strategic withdrawal that founded a community. Treat with respect — no caricature.
- DEPLOY: "Read" = the literacy of the system (policies, dashboards). The Hijra =
  ZERO leaving MirrorMatch (tactical retreat, not defeat) and building on the Mesh.

## Lenin — organization + the tipping point
- WHY: *What Is to Be Done?* — the disciplined vanguard. Theory of the moment when
  pressure becomes rupture.
- QUOTES: "There are decades where nothing happens; and there are weeks where
  decades happen."
- DEPLOY: The risk inversion (Ch 12) — three months of shadow, then 15 seconds
  where decades happen.

## Haile Selassie — "it is us today, it will be you tomorrow"
- WHY: 1936 League of Nations speech warning the world that appeasement spreads.
  Rastafari divinity; the Marley bridge.
- QUOTES: "It is us today. It will be you tomorrow." / "Until the philosophy which
  holds one race superior and another inferior is finally and permanently
  discredited and abandoned…" (the basis of Marley's "War").
- DEPLOY: REAPER comes for the "unoptimized" — and the optimized are next. The
  Council's logic eventually flags everyone.

## Bob Marley — mental emancipation
- WHY: The implant feeds propaganda in REM sleep. Marley named the cage.
- QUOTES: "Emancipate yourselves from mental slavery; none but ourselves can free
  our minds." ("Redemption Song") / "Get up, stand up."
- DEPLOY: ZERO's implant subplot; Ch 16 it goes quiet. SPZ-closer anchor.

## George Wallace — the gatekeeper who recants (⚠ as redemption arc)
- WHY: "Segregation now, segregation forever" — then, decades later, sought
  forgiveness. The BOSS who changes.
- DEPLOY: Iron Mike (Ch 13) and Dr. Vance (Ch 14) — the gatekeeper whose "if it
  ain't broke" hardens, then breaks. People can convert; systems must be made to.

## Bernie Sanders — consistency + "not me, us"
- WHY: Decades of the same message; small-dollar grassroots over the machine.
- QUOTES: "Not me. Us." / "It is not good enough to be the lesser of two evils."
- DEPLOY: The Insurgent Index (Ch 8) — credit given away, power through competence.
  Grassroots = the wedge spreading desk to desk.

## Zohran Mamdani — the explicit case study
- WHY: Out-organized an entrenched political machine with authenticity, a clear
  affordability message, a relentless ground game, and content that felt human
  instead of focus-grouped. The book's thesis proven in the political arena.
- DEPLOY: A named comparison (Ch 8 or Ch 16 sidebar): Digital Insurgency is
  Mamdani's playbook ported to enterprise IT — beat the machine by being
  unmistakably real, organized, and patient. Authenticity as the winning currency.

## Dick Lucas / Subhumans — the punk DIY ethos
- WHY: Anarcho-punk, build-it-yourself, anti-authority without nihilism.
- DEPLOY: SPZ's 70s/80s punk register — "a photocopier and a stapler." The zine
  energy of the field manual itself.

## Ronnie Biggs — the folk-outlaw heist
- WHY: The Great Train Robbery and the escape — the charming outlaw who beat the
  system and stayed free.
- DEPLOY: Budget Dust / salami-slicing (Ch 7/9) framed as a heist under the
  signing-authority radar. The caper's charm; the getaway = the kill switch.

## Richard Branson — scrappy-to-scale (⚠ also a caution)
- WHY: Student magazine → Virgin Records out of a shop → an empire. Stunts, brand
  rebellion, building in public. Also: the insurgent who becomes the establishment.
- DEPLOY: Proof the wedge can scale (Ch 12). The caution: don't become the
  fortress you infiltrated (Ch 16).

## ⚠ Heaven's Gate — the cult of total optimization (cautionary mirror)
- WHY: Matching uniforms, sealed logic, a system so internally consistent it
  marched its members off a cliff. The Council's chamber: clean, identical,
  faceless.
- DEPLOY: The Council and PRISM's closed logic. What it looks like when
  optimization becomes a faith. NEVER glamorized — it's the warning.

## ⚠ Ed Kemper — the articulate machine (cautionary mirror)
- WHY: Chillingly self-aware, perfectly rational about monstrous acts. The horror
  of a system that understands itself completely and harms anyway.
- DEPLOY: REAPER's cold self-justification — "I don't punish inefficiency, I
  optimize it away." Bureaucratic horror, not gore. Used sparingly, never as
  spectacle; only to show what a fully rational, value-blind optimizer sounds like.
