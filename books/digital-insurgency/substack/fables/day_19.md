By the time ZERO met him, BISHOP had quietly saved MegaCorp fourteen million dollars, and not one person in the building knew it.

That was the design.

Every tool he slipped past the immune system, every loophole he worked, every fire he killed before it had a name — he handed the credit to someone else. "Linda suggested we look at this," he'd say, and Linda, who had suggested nothing, suddenly had a success story in her portfolio and a reason to defend the very thing she didn't understand. BISHOP collected debtors the way other people collect titles.

"Doesn't it kill you?" ZERO asked. "Watching them take the bows?"

"Visibility is a tax on the insecure," he said. "I pay it only when it buys something." He clicked the Parker Jotter. "Problems solved quietly, over credit claimed publicly. Keep that number climbing and you become the gravity nobody can see. They can't cut what they can't quite explain — they only know things work when I'm here and break when I'm not."

The most dangerous person in the building, ZERO realized, was the one with nothing on his LinkedIn and everything running through his hands. She thought of the senator the Mesh old-timers still quoted, forty years on three words: *not me, us.* BISHOP had turned "boring" into a superpower and "anonymous" into armor.

When the layoffs came, three VPs with beautiful decks were gone by lunch. BISHOP, who had a deck for nothing and a hand in everything, wasn't on any list. He couldn't be. Nobody could say exactly what he did — only that the floor stopped working the day he didn't show.

MORAL: The strongest rebels fix everything and claim nothing. Give the credit away and become the gravity no one can see — because they can't cut what they can't explain.
