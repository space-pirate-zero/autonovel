## [THE CHEATSHEET]

> ♪ **SCORE — BRIEFING** · steady briefing bed (soft tick + pad) · a data-stinger per equation as it's named · soft fade out.

SPZ: Act one — the Physics. Ten numbers about whether the ground under you is already
gone.

SPZ: One. The Extinction Rate. Generic lines of code over authentic lines, times your
AI velocity. Generic is anything a model regenerates in seconds; authentic is the code
that carries human judgment. Under one, you're green. One to five, yellow. Over five,
red — you're landfill the moment the tooling catches up. The move: raise the
denominator. Find the weird. Everything generic is already gone.

SPZ: Two. The Authenticity Half-Life. Taste plus context plus craft, all over
replicability. Long half-life — months to years — is green. Weeks is yellow. Under six
months is red. And if your half-life is measured in minutes, you don't have a product.
You have a prompt.

SPZ: Three. The Theater Ratio. Press releases plus demo days, over production deploys.
Under two, green. Two to five, yellow. Over five, red — all talk, no ship. Count their
deploys, not their announcements. The Innovation Lab is a thermodynamic scam. Heat in,
zero work out.

SPZ: Four. Vendor Gravity Force. Sunk cost times political capital, over migration
effort *squared.* Squared because risk compounds when you rip out something embedded.
Under twenty, green — it's movable. Twenty to eighty, yellow. Over eighty, red — do not
pitch a replacement. High gravity means be a sidecar, not a rip-and-replace. You can't
fight the well. Orbit it.

SPZ: Five. The Entropy Tax. Maintenance, times e to the power of lambda-times-time.
Don't read a point, read the curve. Flat is green, bending is yellow, hockey stick is
red. The status quo is never free. Run the financial autopsy and show them the bill
they're already paying.

SPZ: Six. The Legacy Friction Coefficient. Years since the last update, over pages of
documentation. Low is green, mid is yellow, high is red — obscure and terrifying means
nobody will touch it. And high friction is *your* opening. Be the only one who
understands it.

SPZ: Seven. The Technical Debt Compound Rate. Bugs found over bugs fixed. Under
point-eight, green. Point-eight to one, yellow. Over one, red — the team is underwater.
Don't pitch a blueprint to a drowning person. Be the bucket.

SPZ: Eight. The Consensus Paralysis Factor. n times n-minus-one, over two — the number
of communication paths in the room. Four people or fewer, that's six paths, green. Five
to seven people, ten to twenty-one paths, yellow. Eight or more, over twenty-eight
paths, red. Keep the room small. Committees are graveyards. Every added head multiplies
the paths to "no" and adds none to "yes."

SPZ: Nine. The Curator Value Function. Signal selected over noise available, times
context depth. It explodes as the noise goes to infinity — but only if the context is
real. If your context depth is near zero, red: you're a Top-Ten listicle. Sell the
taste layer, not the generator. They have infinite generation and zero trust.

SPZ: Ten. The Authenticity Token. Human trust, times one over AI substitutability.
High trust and low substitutability — a sommelier's pick — is green. Toward zero, red:
AI-washable. Prove you have context a model can't fake. That's the whole moat.

SPZ: Act two — the Protocol. Ten numbers about getting inside without tripping the
alarm.

SPZ: Eleven. The Risk Tax. Probability of failure times the cost of failure, plus panic
time times career cost. Under a hundred, green. A hundred to a thousand, yellow. Over a
thousand, red — the career risk outweighs any upside, so walk. You're not selling
features. You're lowering the number running in their amygdala. Flip the cortisol
source — make the status quo the scary option.

SPZ: Twelve. Decision Fatigue. Decisions made over glucose remaining. Before eleven AM,
green. Post-lunch, yellow. After two PM, red — the default answer is "no." Pitch in the
morning. Saying "no" costs zero calories. Bring food if you have to go late.

SPZ: Thirteen. The Dorian Gray Index. Dark-pattern revenue over trust eroded, times
time. Under ten, green. Ten to fifty, yellow. Over fifty, red — the portrait is becoming
visible, collapse incoming. Trust erodes exponentially, not linearly. Put this number
next to the growth dashboard. Some execs look. Those are your allies.

SPZ: Fourteen. The Soul Score. Sum, across every feature, of human impact times honest
intent — and intent is binary, plus one if it's genuine, minus one if it's exploitative.
Net positive, green. Net negative, red — fix the product before you read another page. If
it's negative, you're a mercenary, not an insurgent. It's the Mirror Test, quantified.

SPZ: Fifteen. The Reversibility Ratio. Blast impact over rollback speed. Under one,
green — ship it. One to ten, yellow — feature-flag it. Over ten, red — canary first, or
don't deploy. Build the kill switch before the feature. Hand the CIO the button and say,
press it.

SPZ: Sixteen. Wedge Velocity. Acute pain times trust increment, over adoption friction.
Over fifty, green — it goes viral on its own. Ten to fifty, yellow. Under one, red — it's
a platform pretending to be a wedge. If adoption needs procurement, legal, or IT
permission, your wedge is blunt. Find the bleeding neck.

SPZ: Seventeen. The Good Trouble Coefficient. Impact of the change times how cleanly it
aligns with your values, over risk plus friction. Over five, green — go. Two to five,
yellow — find a shadow sponsor. Under one, red — vanity, wrong terrain. And if the values
alignment is zero, the whole thing collapses no matter the impact. Good trouble requires
good intent. It's structural, not sentimental.

SPZ: Eighteen. The Insurgent Index. Problems solved quietly, over credit claimed
publicly. Toward infinity, green — maximum power. Toward zero, red — you're building a
LinkedIn, not a movement. Give the credit away. Make your manager the hero. They become
your shield.

SPZ: Nineteen. Legacy Liability. Principal times one-plus-r, to the power of t — with r,
the decay constant, running around fifteen to twenty percent. Read the three-year,
five-year, ten-year projection. Anything that doubles inside five years is red. The
cheapest option always looks expensive today. Show them what doing nothing costs.

SPZ: Twenty. The Self-Funding Coefficient. Human cost minus AI cost, over pilot cost.
Over five, green — it pays for itself. Over ten, double-green — the CFO is negligent not
to buy. Under one, red — don't pitch the CFO yet. Below five, go back to the user buyer
and expand the use case until it clears.

SPZ: Act three — the Invasion. Four numbers about taking the throne.

SPZ: Twenty-one. The Zombie Scorecard. Pain-owner times five, plus timeline times three,
plus budget-code times five. Max score sixty-five. Forty-five to sixty-five, green — it's
alive. Twenty-five to forty-four, yellow — one vital sign's missing. Zero to twenty-four,
red — it's a corpse; send the breakup email. The zombie is usually the gatekeeper, not
the company. Walk away and watch who calls back.

SPZ: Twenty-two. The Vaporware Index. Adjectives in the pitch over live demo minutes.
Under five, green. Five to ten, yellow. Over ten, red — slides, not software. And it
works both ways: score yourself. Cut adjectives, add demo.

SPZ: Twenty-three. The Audit Defense Score. Certifications times scope, over auditor
fear. Weight the certs — FedRAMP counts ten, SOC 2 Type Two counts seven, ISO counts
six, a pen test counts three, a self-assessment counts one. Over fifty, green — rubber
stamp. Twenty to fifty, yellow. Under five, red — rejection. Make saying no riskier than
saying yes. Bring the comparison matrix. Inherit compliance from your cloud. Lower the
auditor's fear to zero.

SPZ: Twenty-four. The Trust over Risk Ratio — the one that earns the throne. Volume of
real traffic times days of uptime, over the weighted severity of your incidents. Over
ten thousand, green — ready to be the primary. Over a hundred thousand, double-green — you
are the System of Record. Under a hundred, red — stay in the shadow. Run silent until the
math is undeniable, then push the alert. The risk inverts. Turning you *off* becomes the
dangerous move.

SPZ: Twenty-four numbers. None of them care what you meant. Run them before you write
another line, take another meeting, or ship another feature.

## [THE INFILTRATION CANVAS]

> ♪ **SCORE — BRIEFING** · briefing bed continues · a page-turn click between each Canvas field · soft fade out.

SPZ: Now the second thing in the bag. The Infiltration Canvas. One page per engagement,
eight panels, in order. Answer every prompt in one line. And here's the rule that makes
it work: if a field comes up blank, that blank *is* your next mission task. A Canvas with
three blanks is a deal you are not ready to run. Walk it with me, panel by panel.

SPZ: Panel one — Environment. The physics. Ask: what kind of gravity well am I walking
into? Run Vendor Gravity — replace, or sidecar? Run the Entropy Tax — what's the status
quo costing per day, and is that curve bending? Check the legacy friction and the debt
compound — is this team building, or drowning? Check the theater ratio — do they ship, or
do they announce? Finish the panel with one line: this account is a high-, or low-,
gravity well, and the right entry is *this.*

SPZ: Panel two — People. The factions. Who owns the pain, the budget, and the veto? Name
your technocrat, the architecture gatekeeper — their fear, and what wins them. Name your
bureaucrat, the process and security wall — their fear, and what wins them. Name your
shadow warlord, the one with off-org-chart budget. Name your champion — the user buyer,
the bleeding neck — and what makes *them* want to quit their job on a Friday night. Find
your shadow sponsor, the senior cover, or admit you don't have one yet and go get one.
And name the Three Ghosts: which past vendor, migration, or cloud trauma still haunts
this room? One line to close: enter through *this* person, pitch *this* one for money,
*this* one for credibility, and bribe *this* one with compliance artifacts.

SPZ: Panel three — Qualification. Is it alive, or am I selling to a corpse? Run the
Zombie Score. Name the pain owner — the actual human who bleeds from this specific
problem. Name the timeline — a real by-when with a real consequence. Name the budget code
— does your contact control the money, or sit one desk from it? And run the vaporware
check on your *own* pitch. One line to close: green, yellow, or red — and if it's red,
send the breakup email and move your blood somewhere it circulates.

SPZ: Panel four — Camouflage. Architecture as disguise. Does my radical thing look boring
enough to survive the review board? Write your relabels — "AI Inference Layer" becomes
something the board has approved a hundred times before. Count your boxes — six or fewer,
all names they already trust? Name your strangler phase — intercept, observe, route, or
strangle? And write the inception script: which Socratic questions lead them to draw your
diagram themselves? One line to close: the board will read this as *this boring thing* and
approve it without ever seeing the *real thing* inside.

SPZ: Panel five — Shield. Weaponized bureaucracy. Can I make saying no riskier than
saying yes? Run the Audit Defense Score. List the certs you hold, and the ones you can
inherit from your cloud. Is your synthetic data ready — the thing that kills the privacy
review? Build the comparison matrix: on how many control points do you beat the
incumbent? One line to close: keeping the legacy vendor is now the *risky* choice,
because *this.*

SPZ: Panel six — Math. The economics of safety. What's the one number that ends the
argument? Run Legacy Liability at seventeen percent — the three-year, five-year, ten-year
figure. Run the Self-Funding Coefficient — you need it above five. Write the one-sheet:
the daily cost of inaction, in dollars per day and dollars per year. Find the budget
pocket — CapEx or OpEx, which quarter, who signs under the threshold? One line to close:
doing nothing costs *this many* dollars a day. Here's the fire under your chair.

SPZ: Panel seven — the Invasion Plan. Shadow to System of Record. How do I go from
experiment to oxygen? Run the Reversibility Ratio — your kill switch reverts in how many
milliseconds? Run Wedge Velocity — you need it above ten, adoption friction pinned to one.
Set your Trust-over-Risk target before you go live — above ten thousand to be the primary,
above a hundred thousand to be the System of Record. Four phases: shadow read-only, then
canary at one to five percent, then strangle by routing traffic by type, then System of
Record. And name your risk-inversion trigger: what crisis makes turning you *off* the
dangerous move? One line to close: I run silent until the Trust-over-Risk is undeniable,
then I push the alert.

SPZ: Panel eight — the Pre-Mortem. The Mirror Test, saved for last on purpose. Assume
it's a year later and this failed — or worse, it succeeded and it shouldn't have. Run the
Soul Score: is what I'm smuggling medicine, or poison? Name the empathy gap: whose fear
did I fail to neutralize? Face the Keisha risk: if this gets somebody fired, is it me
protecting them — or me protecting me? Run the Good Trouble Coefficient one last time,
above two or don't. One line to close: if this works, the portrait heals by *this much*;
if it doesn't, the failure mode is *this.*

SPZ: That's the Canvas. In one breath: map the gravity, name the factions, confirm the
deal has a pulse, wear the camouflage, carry the shield, bring the one number, run the
invasion silent until the math flips — and before any of it, pass the Mirror Test. Eight
panels, one page. If you can't fill it, you're not ready to infiltrate. You're ready to
get ejected.

## [THE SPZ CLOSER]

> ♪ **SCORE — CLOSER** · everything drops; a warm chord blooms (the "sit-down" cue) · confessional bed: vinyl crackle + lone Rhodes + Waffle-House room tone · crackle holds under the last word.

SPZ: [softly] That's the kit. That's everything I've got. Fourteen drops, twenty-four
numbers, one Canvas, and a woman who walked out of a server room at three-fourteen in
the morning and never badged back in.

SPZ: I want to tell you the truth about these equations before I let you go. They are
not magic. Half of them I scrawled on the back of a Waffle House placemat trying to
explain to myself why the smartest thing I ever built got killed in a committee by
people who never read it. The numbers didn't save that project. Nothing was going to
save that project. But the numbers taught me why it died — and the *next* one lived,
because I ran silent, I found the bleeding neck, I gave the credit away, and I signed
nothing.

SPZ: The kit doesn't make you brave. It just takes away your excuses. When the Trust
over Risk is deep green and you still won't push the alert, that's not caution anymore.
That's the implant talking. When the Good Trouble scores in the thousands and you still
want the fire, that's the ego, not the mission. The math strips the story off your fear
and shows you what it actually is. That's the whole gift. It's an ugly gift. Take it
anyway.

SPZ: You've got the physics. You've got the protocol. You've got the Canvas and the
Oath and a portrait that's allowed to heal now. The only thing left is the part I can't
hand you in a bag. You have to go *look* at your engine. And then you have to go build
the real thing, four percent at a time, while everyone louder than you burns bright and
reboots by morning.

SPZ: Deface the currency. Smuggle the real thing inside the counterfeit. Give it away.
And when the going gets weird — and brother, it is already so weird — turn pro.

## [FIELD ASSIGNMENT]

> ♪ **SCORE — ASSIGNMENT** · one decisive percussive hit + a rising two-note "go" figure · short punchy CTA motif (~10–15s) · button, then silence.

SPZ: One last assignment. The only one that matters now, because it's the one you'll do
without me. Pick one real target this week — a real account, a real fortress, a real
problem with a real human bleeding inside it. And fill the Infiltration Canvas for it.
All eight panels. One page. One line each.

SPZ: Every blank you can't fill is a mission task with your name on it. Map the gravity.
Name the factions. Check for a pulse. Cut the camouflage. Load the shield. Bring the one
number. Plan the silent invasion. And pass the Mirror Test before you move a muscle.

SPZ: [softly] Fill the Canvas. Then go run it. [pause] There's no "next time" — this is
the last transmission. But there's a next *you*, the one who ships the real thing while
the building sleeps. Go be her. Go be him. Go be the clerk who outlives the building.

SPZ: This has been *Digital Insurgency.* I'm Space Pirate Zero, and I'll be out here on
the salvage decks where the static's thickest, listening for you. Signal finds signal.
