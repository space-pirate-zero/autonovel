Decision quality collapses after ~4 hours of deciding.

By 2 PM the average exec has made 200 calls and defaults to the lowest-energy option, which is always 'no' or 'defer.' Never pitch after lunch. 'No' costs zero calories.

The moral of the story: Decision quality is a fuel gauge, not a personality. A tired brain defaults to no. Bring your ask to a full tank — the morning, not the dregs of the afternoon.
