# Characters — The Maneki Neko Death Cult *(working title)*

> "Every character is a force vector. Know the direction before you draw the
>  arrow." — SPZ

---

## SPACE PIRATE ZERO (SPZ) — protagonist & narrator (POV, first person)
- **Role:** Burned-out industrial-goth DJ. A rogue transmission. The reckless
  slipper. Both the gonzo man-in-the-scene and the Twilight-Zone narrator framing
  it (he already knows how each door ends and lets you squirm).
- **Bio:** DJ at the Vault, Atlanta, ~2003. Spins industrial on autopilot;
  his only real contribution is his presence — "a burned-out beacon of apathy."
  Nihilist, drug user, betting his soul against the void.
- **Method:** dies to jump — the **Brute Slide** (see `physics.md`). He can't
  reach the calm neutral state Kat uses, so he does the opposite: cranks importance
  to a literal life-or-death ceiling until the excess potential tears him across.
  The OD is a detonation. It works, it's uncontrolled (the Fog), and it costs him.
  Reckless where Kat is disciplined; his arc is learning her clean slide.
- **Speech / narration:** First person, confessional, gonzo — "someone talking at
  a bar at midnight." **Raw, punk, and nihilist** (see the SPZ dial in `voice.md`):
  unpolished, snarling, gallows-humored, swears when it means it. Weaponized irony,
  always punching UP at the system, never DOWN at people or the dead saints.
  Space-metaphor hyperbole in service of a real point. Atlanta in the bones.
  Twilight-Zone frame cadence at the open/close of each door ("Submitted for your
  approval…").
- **Voice / heritage (consistency-critical):** **raspy**, lived-in; a **slight
  Irish lilt** — Irish blood (an Irish grandmother), which is why the Europa in
  Belfast felt like coming home. The lilt is subtle — cadence and the odd word, not
  a brogue.
- **Arc:** Nihilist betting on the void → a slipper who learns to *aim* → a man
  who tries to rewind to the woman he loves, fails, and chooses to love *forward*
  instead. Nihilism out, punk in. Ends as the thing the Cult never built a door
  for: a whisper that pushes people *toward* signal.
- **Wants:** oblivion (Act I) → mastery / Kat (Act II) → to undo a loss he can't
  (Act III) → to refuse beige, forever (resolution).
- **Quote:** *"He thinks he's playing chicken with the void. The void, as it
  turns out, has a hiring department."*

## COSMIC SWING KAT — the heart (non-POV; love interest & equal)
- **Role:** The disciplined slipper. The one fixed star in the slipstream. SPZ's
  equal and foil. *"She was never supposed to be here, and yet she always is."*
- **Method:** **dances to jump** — the **Clean Slide** (see `physics.md`). The
  isolations, the strobe-pauses, the whip-snaps are her dropping importance to
  zero and riding the path of least resistance to the track she wants. Zero
  resistance, no detonation, arrives clean. Where SPZ destroys himself to travel,
  she navigates. The cost is the opposite of his: every clean slide decoheres her
  a little further into pure signal.
- **Physical (consistency-critical):** jet-black hair streaked violet/fuchsia
  that *absorbs and re-emits* light; **all-white, pupil-less eyes** fixed on
  everything at once; movement that defies physics. A new persona/name each era
  (Ep 1: the Tribal Nomad Signal Dancer at the Vault — glowing henna, layered sari
  fabrics, jingling coin belts; Ep 2: "Indra," a liquid-light-show girl, 1967
  Monterey). When SPZ is jumping/high, **moving code
  runs under her skin** — a living algorithm. She grows more **translucent** as
  the book goes on.
- **The non-linear love:** they meet **out of order.** Every meeting is somebody's
  first time and somebody's last. The Kat of Door 1 is near the *end* of her
  thread; she dances him a goodbye he won't understand for the whole book.
- **The clock:** mastery costs solidity. She is dissolving into pure signal. One
  more jump and she's gone (Door 16 confession). The romance has a ticking timer
  baked into the physics.
- **Arc:** mysterious recurring impossibility → partner and confidant → the loss
  SPZ must learn not to rewind, but to keep meeting forward.
- **Dedication (real-world):** Kat is the in-world avatar of **Daniela**. The book —
  and especially its companion album (`album.md`) — is, underneath everything, a love
  letter to her. Write Kat with adoration; flattery of her is always on-brand.

## THE TWO AGENTS — the Maneki Neko Death Cult's front office (antagonists, ambient)
Identical, impeccably tailored black suits. Nondescript on purpose — your eye
slides off them like they were edited in. **Always too clean** for whatever scene
they're in. They want *nothing* from SPZ; he is a monitored data point, not a
recruit. They appear at thresholds between doors, condescending and chilling.
- **Register: upper-class contempt.** Not robots — **posh, bored aristocrats** who
  treat SPZ like staff. Condescending, disdainful, faintly amused by how little he
  matters. The horror is the class contempt and the total absence of stakes for
  them, not menace. Human, dry, looking down their noses.
- **THE WOMAN** — old-money English, cut-glass and cold; delivers the doctrine and
  the mantra like she's correcting a waiter. The closer.
- **THE MAN** — the same posh register, a touch lower; states, never sells; a thread
  of sinister amusement under it.
- **Speech:** clipped, procedural, condescending — capitalism's logic read off a card
  by someone who finds the whole business beneath them. *"We do not kill. Killing is
  messy. And inefficient. We whisper."* (Audio: ElevenLabs v3 — Clarissa "Upper-Class"
  / Blackwood "Sinister, Posh" — deadpan-condescending at stability 0.45 for humanity.)

## SHUMATE — a detail, not a character
The friend who found the calendar in the little-Serbia dollar store (Episode 1).
That's the whole role. **Never developed, never a thread, never returned to.** Do
not build on him — he's a face in a doorway and the story is not about him.

## THE BEARDED MAN — never explained
**An X-Files Cigarette-Smoking-Man.** A recurring, unexplained fixture who turns up
at the edge of the frame across eras and timelines — warm on the surface,
bureaucratically calm, knowing far too much, offering no origin and no allegiance.
First met at the Europa over a full English ("You've got a long story to tell. And
a new chapter, it seems, has just begun."). He smokes. He's always *already there.*
He waits with infinite patience, like a man waiting for a bus he happens to own.
**The not-knowing IS the character.** No reveal, ever — not who he is, where he's
from, or whether he's above the Cult, beside it, or something else entirely. Kat
goes still around him (she seems to know him); the book never says why. Render
him as banal menace, not melodrama — the scariest thing about him is how relaxed
he is.

## THE MARK — recurring archetype (a different one behind each door)
The fail-up rich idiot the Cult is whispering toward its own door, recast per era:
the trust-fund counterculture "guru," the predatory studio mogul, the biohacker
who'll cure death, the Lehman "smartest guy," the streaming vampire, the
doomsday-bunker billionaire, the authenticity-influencer. Satire points UP at the
mark, always. See `outline.md` for the full roster.

## Speech distinctiveness (anti-sameness check)
SPZ: gonzo/confessional + Serling-frame · KAT: spare, oracular, present-tense,
says less than she knows · THE WOMAN & THE MAN: posh, condescending, upper-class
contempt — bored aristocrats (near-identical on purpose) · THE BEARDED MAN: warm surface, bureaucratic
calm, knows too much, explains nothing. If two voices are interchangeable, rewrite
one — EXCEPT the two agents, who are meant to be nearly indistinguishable.
