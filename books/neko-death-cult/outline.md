# Outline — The Maneki Neko Death Cult *(working title)*

24 doors of the Advent Calendar of Death. Each door = one cocktail (a dead artist
the calendar reveres as a *saint*) = one jump to an era and city = one Twilight
Zone fable. A fail-up **mark** the Maneki Neko Death Cult is whispering toward its own
door. A **sting** that lands back on SPZ. And the thread that carries **Kat**, the
**Cult**, and **beige** across the whole book. Door 1 *is* the source blog. The
satire points UP at the marks; the dead saints are treated with reverence.

**This is a periodical: one door = one episode** (fiction podcast + Substack
series). Each door is engineered to stand alone for a new listener AND pay off the
serial arc — see `format.md` for the episode architecture, the season map (Acts =
Seasons), cadence, and the December "advent" release stunt. `door` = `episode` =
`chapter`. Chapter headers use the pipeline-required `### Ch N:` form so the
drafting tools can find them.

---

## ACT I — THE FOG (Doors 1–8)
*He thinks it's roulette. He's learning it's a map.*

### Ch 1: The Belushi Door
Speedball · the Vault, Atlanta, 2003 → the motel → the Europa, Belfast. The pilot
(the source blog). The OD, the Cult's courtesy call, the first clean landing
handed to him as bait. Kat dances him a goodbye (her thread is near its end). The
mantra lands: *don't let the bastards grind you down to beige.*

### Ch 2: The Joplin Door
Southern Comfort & a needle · Monterey, 1967, the Summer of Love curdling. **Mark:**
a trust-fund "guru" incorporating the counterculture. **Sting:** whispered into a
messianic ego-trip; the IRS finishes what the whisper started. Kat slips him a
note he won't understand for ten doors.

### Ch 3: The Pimp C Door
Lean · Houston/Atlanta, 2007, Dirty South money. **Mark:** a label exec robbing
his own artists. **Sting:** whispered into a chain he can't afford and a feud he
can't survive. First time SPZ sees the *code* moving under Kat's skin, half-speed
in a chopped-and-screwed room.

### Ch 4: The Hendrix Door *(flex slot)*
Wine & barbiturates · Mayfair, London, 1970. **Mark:** an art dealer laundering
money through "genius." **Sting:** a forgery scheme; he chokes on his own greed.

### Ch 5: The Marilyn Door
Nembutal · Old Hollywood, 1962, the studio system. **Mark:** a predatory mogul who
fails up forever. **Sting:** the system he built eats him — and here the reaping
*looks like justice*, which is the first thing that scares SPZ.

### Ch 6: The Morrison Door *(flex slot)*
A Paris bathtub, 1971 · Père Lachaise. **Mark:** an expat Ponzi swindler who fakes
his own death. **Sting:** the Cult lets him fake it, then collects anyway. Kat
leaves him a grave rubbing.

### Ch 7: The Hemingway Door
The calendar's one *sealed* door (a shotgun, not a cocktail) · Ketchum/Havana,
1961. **Mark:** a "great man" hedge-fund hunter. **Sting:** the Everest-faulty-gear
method, Hemingway-flavored. The sealed door is planted as SPZ's future
temptation: the one bet you can't come back from.

### Ch 8: The Anna Nicole Door
A prescription cocktail · tabloid Florida/Bahamas, 2007. **Mark:** a reality-TV
fortune-chaser marrying oil money. **Sting:** the **yacht-into-orca-waters**
whisper. Kat appears as the anchor reporting the death — looking straight through
the camera at him. **Act I out:** the Cult is behind every door, and Kat remembers
futures he hasn't lived.

## ACT II — THE MASTERY (Doors 9–16)
*The fog lifts. He learns to aim. She starts to disappear.*

### Ch 9: The Cobain Door
Seattle, 1994, the grunge gold rush. **Mark:** an A&R vulture gutting the bands he
signs. **Sting:** the authenticity he sells is the thing he kills. SPZ's first
*deliberate* landing. Kat — a zine-maker here — finally tells him she dances to
jump. The rule, explained.

### Ch 10: The Elvis Door *(flex slot)*
Memphis, 1977, the bloat of fame. **Mark:** a "Colonel" milking a captive star.
**Sting:** the parasite outlives the host and starves. SPZ and Kat's first real
night — his first time; she won't say which of hers.

### Ch 11: The Whitney Door
Beverly Hilton, 2012, the comeback industry. **Mark:** a biohacker billionaire
who'll "cure death." **Sting:** the **homemade-submarine** method — he dives to
the bottom to live forever and implodes. Kat flickers translucent at the edges;
the dissolution clock starts.

### Ch 12: The Europa Door (II)
Back to Belfast — but *aimed*; his first fully earned clean landing. **Mark:** a
disaster-capitalist peace-process profiteer. **Sting + payoff:** the bearded man
recurs — and is *never explained* (X-Files Cigarette-Smoking-Man; canon.md). The
real payoff is **Kat's reaction** to him (she goes still, like she knows him) and
her **Door-2 note** finally landing. The mystery deepens; it does not resolve.

### Ch 13: The Lenny Bruce Door
NYC, 1966, the obscenity trials. **Mark:** a moral-panic media mogul monetizing
outrage. **Sting:** the Cult's whisper *backfires* — turns his target into a
martyr, *more* signal, not less. First time SPZ watches the Cult fail, and grins.
The doubt crystallizes: do they prune beige, or manufacture it?

### Ch 14: The Sid Vicious Door
Chelsea Hotel, 1979, punk eating itself. **Mark:** a manager selling rebellion as
merch. **Sting:** SPZ openly *interferes* for the first time, saves someone the
Cult marked — and becomes visible to them as more than a data point.

### Ch 15: The Heath Ledger Door
NYC, 2008, the crash. **Mark:** a Lehman "smartest guy in the room." **Sting:** the
2008 collapse *was* a mass culling — the Cult's biggest reap. Scale-horror. Kat
spends her own solidity to drag him out of a bad landing.

### Ch 16: The Amy Winehouse Door
Camden, 2011. **Mark:** a streaming/paparazzi vampire monetizing a person's
decline. **Sting:** *the audience is the Cult now* — everyone whispering. Midpoint
gut-punch: beige isn't blandness, it's **the crowd flattening a human into
content.** Kat's confession: one more jump and she's gone. **Act II out.**

## ACT III — THE REFUSAL (Doors 17–24)
*He can't rewind. He has to choose forward.*

### Ch 17: The Michael Jackson Door *(flex slot)*
2009, the comeback that kills. **Mark:** a handler selling sleep to a captive.
**Sting:** SPZ tries to **rewind** to a younger Kat — and slams into the
unbreakable rule for real. He loses.

### Ch 18: The Hunter S. Thompson Door
A gunshot; the gonzo saint · Aspen/Vegas, 2005. **Mark:** a "disruptor" media
baron. **Sting:** the influence payoff — *buy the ticket, take the ride.*
Temptation peaks: SPZ eyes the sealed Hemingway door as a shortcut to Kat.

### Ch 19: The Davos Door
Modern; *a blank door he mixes himself* (the first recipe he authors). **Mark:**
the Cult's own **clients.** **Sting:** the reveal — the Maneki Neko Death Cult takes
contracts *from the system.* They aren't beige's cure. They're its quality
control.

### Ch 20: The Dubai Door
Oligarch excess. **Mark:** a space-tourism / doomsday-bunker billionaire. **Sting:**
the rich engineering their exit from the planet they wrecked; the Cult just holds
the door. SPZ refuses a reap for the first time — walks away from an easy win.

### Ch 21: The GG Allin Door
The saint he feared; the rawest scene. **Mark:** an "authenticity influencer"
cosplaying transgression for clout — beige in a punk costume. **Sting:** he sees
the line between signal and its merch. Nihilism out, punk in. The turn toward the
light.

### Ch 22: The River Phoenix Door
Viper Room, 1993, the inherited curse. **Mark:** a nepo-dynasty failing up across
generations. **Sting:** beige is *hereditary* — privilege as entropy. Kat returns,
near-pure signal, for what may be the last solid night.

### Ch 23: The Sealed Door
The Hemingway bet, finally faced — the one jump you can't come back from. The real
choice under the whole book: dissolve into signal *with* Kat, or stay solid and
lose her to it. The rewind he's wanted was never possible; the only way is
through.

### Ch 24: Door Zero (The Open Door)
The refusal. He doesn't take the bet to chase her backward. He chooses to keep
jumping *forward* — to stay signal in a beige universe, trusting that signal finds
signal, that he and Kat will keep colliding out of order, and that this isn't the
tragedy. It's the love. Dawn at the Europa, the full English, *a new chapter.* The
mantra reclaimed and turned: now **he's** the whisper — a reaper who pushes people
*toward* signal. The thing the Cult never built a door for. Final image mirrors
Door 1.
