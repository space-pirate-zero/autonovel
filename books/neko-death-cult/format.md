# Serial Format & Production — The Maneki Neko Death Cult *(working title)*

This is a **periodical**: a serialized fiction podcast + Substack series. **One
jump (one door) = one episode.** 24 doors = the full run. Each episode is a
self-contained Twilight Zone fable *and* a chapter of one accumulating story.

`door` = `episode` = `chapter` — same unit, three names for three audiences.
Episode titles: **"Episode N — The [Saint] Door"** (e.g., *Episode 1 — The
Belushi Door*). Searchable, collectible, calendar-shaped.

---

## Why the format already fits
The book was designed as a Twilight Zone anthology: every door opens with a frame,
tells a complete fable, and closes on a sting. That is *exactly* an episode. The
serial spine (Kat, the Cult reveal, the discovery staircase, BEIGE) rides
underneath as the reward for followers. We don't restructure — we just tighten the
episode shape and add a serial **button** at the end of each.

## The Episode Architecture (repeatable beat sheet — EVERY door)
1. **Cold open / the hook** (~150 words / 30–60s audio). SPZ drops you mid-scene
   or mid-confession with a grabber line. **No "previously on."**
2. **The Frame (Serling).** SPZ names the era, the mark, and the whisper —
   *"Submitted for your approval…"* This is the onboarding: a brand-new listener
   gets everything they need to enjoy a standalone story.
3. **The Scene (Thompson).** The jump lands. Gonzo, present-tense, reckless. The
   Cult's whisper plays out on the mark; Kat threads through (out of order). This
   is the body — the complete fable.
4. **The Sting.** The mark's self-inflicted comeuppance, and the turn back onto
   SPZ — what this door cost *him*, what it taught him about beige.
5. **The Frame close.** SPZ names the moral without flattening it.
6. **The Button (the serial hook).** 2–4 lines that seed the arc — a Kat mystery,
   a Cult clue, an unresolved thread — that rewards followers and pulls them to the
   next door. This is the only piece that's *serial* rather than *anthology*. It
   must land, every time.

## Show intro + the door's song (SERIES STANDARD — added after Ep1–3)
Every episode is topped and scored the same way. Production order is always
**write the chapter → write & generate the door's song → assemble.** Chapters first.

1. **Show intro (≤20 s).** A fixed, branded cold-open that explains the show in
   general — same voice, ~same words, every episode. Recorded: `audio/show_intro.mp3`
   (19.4 s, SPZ voice):
   > "The Maneki Neko Death Cult. I'm Space Pirate Zero. I die for a living — one
   > door, one dead saint at a time — then wake somewhere new, chasing a girl made of
   > light, running from a cult of polite monsters. Twenty-four doors. Mind the beige."
2. **Song intro.** The door's song (`album.md`) opens the episode — its first
   ~20–30 s play as the theme before the narration begins.
3. **Song as score.** An instrumental / ducked version of that same song is the
   recurring underscore beneath the whole episode — this **REPLACES the generic AI
   beds** (the old `[[MUSIC]]` cues become "bring the theme up / down"). The theme
   returns under key beats and swells at the sting and outro.

Assembly per episode = **[show intro] → [song intro] → [narration + song-as-score]**.
Mixer change (to build once the door's song exists): `mix.py` takes the song file,
lays its head as the intro, and uses a ducked instrumental as the bed instead of
generating beds. Songs are generated by the user in Suno from the `album.md` prompts.

## Self-contained but serial — the rules
- **Start anywhere.** A listener who begins at Episode 7 gets a whole, satisfying
  story. The frame carries all required context, in character.
- **No recap dumps.** Re-establish through SPZ's narration, not exposition:
  *"Last time I died, I…"* The Serling frame is the onboarding device — that's why
  the voice was built this way.
- **Followers get more.** The Kat romance, the discovery staircase (he learns the
  OD = a jump across Doors 1–8), and the Cult reveal (Door 19) accumulate. The
  button is where that debt gets paid and re-loaded.
- **Consistent runtime.** Target **~4,500–5,500 words ≈ ~30 min narrated** (series
  standard). Use the extra length over a bare plot for **world-building** — scene,
  lore, the physics, the Cult's history, Kat's mystery. Same
  shape, same length, every week. Podcast discipline = audience trust.

## Season structure (maps to the three acts)
- **Season 1 — THE FOG** (Episodes 1–8). Ends on the Door 8 break-point: he admits
  he's traveling, not dreaming. Mid-arc cliffhanger finale.
- **Season 2 — THE MASTERY** (Episodes 9–16). Ends on the Door 16 confession: one
  more jump and Kat is gone.
- **Season 3 — THE REFUSAL** (Episodes 17–24). Ends on Door 24: the refusal,
  reclaimed at the Europa.

## Cadence
- **Default: weekly.** One door a week, in three 8-episode seasons with breaks
  between. Sustainable; builds an audience; gives natural season finales at 8/16/24.
- **Signature stunt: the December Advent run.** The in-world object IS an advent
  calendar — so release **one door a day, Dec 1–24**: *"An Advent Calendar of
  Death. One door a day until Christmas. Don't open them all at once."* Use it to
  launch (binge Season 1) or as an annual ritual. This is the marketing hook that
  writes itself.

## Two formats, one source
- **Substack (text + audio).** Each episode ships as the written fable. Tiering:
  free episode, paid gets the audio, early access, the "saint's recipe" liner
  notes, and the cut Kat scenes. Repo precedent: the Digital Insurgency fables
  already shipped via `build_posts.py` / `push_substack.py` / the scheduler —
  adapt, don't rebuild.
- **Podcast (audio).** Narrated first-person — the voice was *built* for this.
  Distribute by RSS to Apple/Spotify. Repo precedent: `gen_audiobook*.py` for
  narration. **Production standard: see `audio.md`** — every prose episode gets
  converted to a tagged ElevenLabs script `chapters/ch_NN.audio.md` (fixed voice
  cast, `@SPEAKER` tags, v3 delivery tags, `<break>` pacing, SFX/music cues).
  Reference template: `chapters/ch_01.audio.md`. Options: a recurring cold-open sound signature (a paper door tearing
  + a needle drop), an industrial/techno ambient bed under the scenes (the world is
  music-soaked), and the mantra as an outro tag.

## Per-episode production checklist
saint+cocktail · era+city (must agree, rule 3) · the mark (punch up) · the Cult
whisper/method · Kat beat (out of order) · the sting back on SPZ · one beige
observation · **the button (serial hook)** · runtime ~30 min ·
cold-open hook in the first 60 seconds.
