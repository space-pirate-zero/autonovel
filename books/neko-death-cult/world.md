# World Bible — The Maneki Neko Death Cult *(working title)*

A gonzo, time-slipping cyberpunk odyssey. First person. **Space Pirate Zero is
the protagonist and narrator** — not the author persona he plays in *Digital
Insurgency*. Tone = the SPZ confessional voice (see `voice.md`) fused with a
Twilight Zone moral-fable frame: each era is a self-contained tale with a sting.

---

## Logline
A burned-out industrial-goth DJ plays Russian roulette with a dollar-store
Advent Calendar of Death — and learns each overdose doesn't kill him, it slips
him sideways through time. He hacks timelines like jukeboxes and never rewinds
the tape. The Maneki Neko Death Cult — capitalism's self-correcting feedback loop — has
been watching. He's not a target. He's a *data point*. And the only fixed star
in the slipstream is a dancer named Cosmic Swing Kat, whom he keeps meeting out
of order, and can never rewind to.

## Premise (the physics)
- **Death is the only door.** A precisely measured overdose from the calendar =
  one jump. No machine, no warning, no aim — at first.
- **He hacks timelines, never rewinds.** Canon, from the source: *"You stumble
  into timelines, you hack them like jukeboxes, never rewinding the tape."* He
  can only skip forward into a new groove and live with the song that plays.
- **He is a rogue transmission.** The Cult monitors "currents." His nihilism and
  his dance with death send ripples through space-time. He is a signal, not a
  soldier.

## The Slipstream — hard rules (NEVER violate)
1. **Death is the only door.** One measured OD = one jump.
2. **You never rewind the tape.** Forward only, into a new present. He cannot
   return to a moment he has lived. This is unbreakable — it is why every goodbye
   is permanent and why the love story is a tragedy with an engine. Note: this
   binds his *own* thread, not the calendar. It's **track travel, not time travel**
   — every era coexists in the block, so he can land in any *when*; he just can't
   re-enter a groove he's already worn. (See `physics.md`: two clocks.)
3. **You arrive how you died.** The cocktail colors the destination — era, city,
   vibe. The calendar is a steerable-but-crude instrument: a jukebox with sticky
   buttons.
4. **Signal finds signal.** Two slippers tuned to the same frequency keep
   colliding across eras. It is why Kat keeps appearing — and why the Cult
   noticed him.
5. **The fog lifts with mastery.** Early jumps are hallucinatory and unreliable
   (the Cathedral of Wires). Clean landings are *earned*. The Europa clean
   landing in Door 1 was a gift/bait — he spends the book trying to get it back.

## Soft rules (can bend for story)
- The calendar's aim is imprecise; he can nudge a destination but not pin it.
- Kat's method differs from his: **she dances to jump**, he dies to jump. She is
  the disciplined slipper to his reckless one.
- Mastery has a cost: the more control a slipper gains, the less solid they
  become. Kat is further along — she is dissolving into pure signal.

## Cosmology — BEIGE vs SIGNAL
The full speculative engine lives in `physics.md` (the slipstream runs on **Reality
Transurfing** physics welded to hard-SF: infinite pre-filmed tracks, sliding by
inner state, importance/excess potential, the Balancing, pendulums). Short version:

Reality runs on two opposed forces.
- **BEIGE** — entropy toward conformity. The heat-death of the soul. Not death;
  *averaging out* — being flattened into the inoffensive, the optimized, the
  safe, the content-shaped. The true antagonist. *"Don't let the bastards grind
  you down to beige."*
- **SIGNAL / PUNK** — the rogue transmission. Authenticity, chaos, the refusal to
  flatten. SPZ and Kat are carriers.
- The **Maneki Neko Death Cult** sits between them, claiming to fight beige by pruning
  the loud and reckless rich — but pruning is *also* a flattening. The book's
  slow reveal: the Cult is not beige's cure. It is beige's quality control.
- Mechanically (see `physics.md`): the Cult is a **pendulum** that whispers
  *importance* into the over-fortuned until **the Balancing** kills them. They
  inflate; physics collects; they never land the blow. BEIGE = the equilibrium
  state pendulums drive everyone toward (all gradients spent, zero signal).

## Era & Place
Frame opens in **early-2000s Atlanta**; the slip blows the universe open across
decades and cities (see `outline.md` for all 24).

| Place | What it is |
|---|---|
| **The Vault** (Atlanta, ~2003) | Industrial goth club. Exposed brick, rusty pipes, a skeletal building. The DJ booth is "a sweat-slicked cockpit of despair." Home base / launch pad. |
| **The Motel Room** | Liminal interrogation space — disinfectant, cheap coffee. Where the Cult makes contact. A waystation between jumps. |
| **The Europa Hotel** (Belfast) | "The world's most bombed hotel." Where he wakes *clean* — no fog. The first landing that sticks. The threshold into the larger story. Recurs in Door 12 and Door 24. |
| **The Cathedral of Wires** | The jump's "between." The Vault morphs into it mid-OD: broken disco balls as shattered planets, stained glass made of screens, techno-gospel liturgy for the damned. |
| **"Little Serbia"** | The dollar store where the calendar was found. A pocket of the old world. |

## Factions & Entities

### The Maneki Neko Death Cult
- **What:** *"Capitalism's feedback loop. The Darwinian force correcting the error
  of greedy, privileged, mainly white executives who fail up."* The cosmic
  sanitation crew.
- **Method — they don't kill, they whisper.** They are the **"Rustpin in the
  ear"** — the suggestion that leads the over-fortuned to make the bet only an
  idiot makes: yacht into orca waters, homemade submarine into the abyss, Everest
  with faulty gear, gas-station boner pills with heart meds. *Self-inflicted*
  Darwinism.
- **Theology:** the **Maneki Neko** (beckoning cat, gatherer of fortune) fused
  with the **grim reaper**. They redistribute the fortune of those who "no longer
  deserve it" back into the soil — garbage collection as blessing, cosmic
  compost.
- **Iconography:** grinning reaper + scythe / beckoning cat / raised paw —
  flashing red → rainbow → unsettling plaid ("cosmic tartan woven from threads
  of fate").
- **Mantra:** *"Don't let the bastards grind you down to beige."*
- See `characters.md` for the two agents.

### The Advent Calendar of Death
Repurposed dollar-store chocolate calendar from little Serbia. **24 doors.** Each
door = a meticulously measured death cocktail, the exact formula that ended a
specific artist (the calendar's tragic *saints*, never punchlines). Functionally:
SPZ's jump console. One sealed door (Hemingway — a shotgun, not a cocktail): the
bet you can't come back from. See `outline.md`.

## Motifs (the texture)
- **Cosmic similes for squalor** — dying stars, collapsing galaxies, comets
  breaking apart, poisoned planets. The grimy rendered as celestial.
- **Beige as the enemy** — conformity as a fate worse than death.
- **Analog-media metaphors** — the needle, the jukebox, the tape, the
  transmission. Timeline manipulation as record-spinning.
- **Techno-gospel** — sound as liturgy; the DJ as a failed priest.
- **Too-clean vs squalor** — the Cult and the Europa are unnaturally clean; the
  jump is the dirty membrane between.

## Internal consistency rules (evaluator checks)
- **Never rewind** is never broken. No do-overs, ever.
- Kat's body shows moving **code** under the skin when SPZ is jumping/high; she
  grows more translucent across the book as she dissolves into signal.
- The Cult **never kills directly** — only whispers. Deaths are always
  self-inflicted by the mark.
- The Cult agents are **always too clean** for whatever scene they're in.
- Time, place, and the calendar-saint must agree per door (rule 3: arrive how you
  died).
- Punch UP at the fail-up marks and the system; never DOWN at the dead saints or
  at ordinary people.
