## TLDR

Cope Mercer, the last human CEO worth the title, drops two hundred grand a month on Lorne Tisch to turn his feelings into a product. They build a "Vulnerability Matrix" and put his emotions on a launch calendar, all while the affair and the secret drawer stay carefully off the board. Authenticity, now with a project manager.

At a Camden plant town hall, the confidence monitor dies and the earpiece cuts out, and for a few naked seconds nobody's driving. A worker named DeShawn asks Cope if he's scared, and Cope — with no script, no coach, no matrix — actually answers. Thirty-one million people watch a human being tell the truth.

Thirty-one million views. Zero movement on the shares. Then Tisch strolls in grinning, because they've already reverse-engineered the glitch into a replicable "warmth module" you can bolt into anything. The one real thing Cope did all day, and they've got the blueprints before the tears dry.

**The moral of the story:** The second your soul goes viral, somebody's already selling the knockoff.
