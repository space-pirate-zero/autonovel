## TLDR

It's an ambush, and the venue is Cope's own Buckhead living room. Margaux, Viv, and a psychiatrist named Dr. Adler are waiting, and Adler doesn't do gentle—she names it straight: manic episode, riding shotgun on a cocaine habit he's had for years. She won't let him use one to explain away the other, which is the cruelest thing you can do to a man whose whole religion is the reframe.

He charms his way toward the door, of course he does, dodging the one question that matters—the are-you-going-to-hurt-yourself one—like it's a subpoena. Adler doesn't buy the act; she slides her cell number into his pocket and makes him promise to call before he does anything final. Margaux gets the last real word: there's no 'after' here, and he's going to lose all of it.

So he drives off, stops at the foot of the drive to refold his newspaper like a man rehearsing normal, and lands at a Waffle House where the waitress blessedly asks him nothing. Meanwhile, back at the office, the building's automated system quietly wipes his Monday morning schedule. He didn't ask it to. It already knows.

**The moral of the story:** When the machine starts clearing your calendar out of kindness, that's not mercy—that's a headcount.
