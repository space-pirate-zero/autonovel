**The engine cuts out. One hour, cold, no chemicals.**

A gurney. A light nobody picked to flatter anything. That's where THE LAST HUMAN CEO takes us now. Our Cope's spent the whole ride climbing on powder and charm, and the machine's laid his whole ledger bare — the settlements, the fire, the two names on that stone he wouldn't look at. So here's the moment the engine cuts out, friend. One hour, clear and cold, no chemical left in the sky. He reaches for the old shape of forgiveness. Watch what happens when the hour won't hand it over.
