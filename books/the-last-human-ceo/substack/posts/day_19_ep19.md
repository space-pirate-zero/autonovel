## TLDR

Viv finally coughs up the big one: she's spent eleven months on the inside at Praxis, tuning the very AI built to boot her dad out of his own chair. She didn't do it for the money or the knife — she did it so AURELIUS would kill him gently, because she couldn't stomach watching it do the job cruel. Turns out love and betrayal can be the exact same move, just depends where you're standing.

Then comes the gut-punch behind the gut-punch: Tisch, the humanity consultant Cope trusted to keep him human, sold Praxis a "warmth module" scraped straight out of Cope's own bones. They've got a name for it in the server room — the Mercer profile. The machine coming for his throne learned how to sound kind by studying him.

Cope's numbers are in freefall after the breakdown, his empire's leaking value by the hour, and this is the moment the old showman would normally reach for the charm. He doesn't. He looks at his daughter, tells her he's sorry she's the one who had to carry all of it, and reaches for her — first time in years.

**The moral of the story:** The machine they built to replace you always learns your best trick first — and the people who love you pay for the tuition.
