**The daughter climbs forty floors with a folded confession.**

Here's a thing they don't put on the awards, friend: a broken man keeps everything. Every putter, every letter, every night he can't sleep — he files it, like a sailor charting a coast gone bad. That's where we are. Cope's swung at a machine and hit only himself, and now the daughter who tried to save the man and not the king climbs forty floors to his door. She's got a confession folded in her pocket. And him? He's already reaching for the knife-shaped version. Stay close.
