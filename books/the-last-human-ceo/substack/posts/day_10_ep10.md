## TLDR

Prescott Cope takes his "Listening Tour" on the road, which is corporate for a photo op where nobody actually listens. He powders his nose with what he calls "discipline," beds embedded journalist Delia March in a Columbus hotel, and generally mistakes momentum for meaning. The man is flying, and not on Delta.

When comms floats a stranger named Darlene for his big "unscripted" good deed, Cope short-circuits — for one ugly second he swears the machine is laughing at him. Then he buries the thought, orders up a more marketable "Steve," and moves on like a coward with a schedule. Nothing says authenticity like recasting the extras.

Margaux calls, but only to report that their daughter Viv snagged the Hauser fellowship, then says goodnight without naming the affair she plainly smells on the line. Cope doesn't call Viv. He lies awake, wired and glowing, feeling more like himself than he has in years — which is exactly the problem.

**The moral of the story:** The scariest thing a hollow man can feel is finally, completely himself.
