## TLDR

Cope Mercer rolls into Defiance, Georgia, to shoot a heartfelt little documentary at the dead family plant, playing hometown hero for the cameras. He works the diner crowd, dazzles Donna and the regulars, and gives a big misty-eyed speech under great-grandpa Aldous's statue. Between takes, he keeps ducking into the bathroom to chemically dial himself "bright" — because nothing says authentic like a man tuning his own frequency.

Down on the river walk, the set dressing cracks. There's a rebuilt house and a memorial stone carved with two names, Karen and Petey Lasko, dated 2022, and Cope makes a point of not looking at it. The camera catches the town; it doesn't catch what he's refusing to see.

On the flight home, his aide Sun sits on something — a memo she wrote back in 2021 and never let go of. She opens her mouth to finally say it, then closes it. The plane keeps flying, and the thing that needed saying stays buried under the engine hum.

**The moral of the story:** You can rebuild the house and re-shoot the speech, but the names on the stone don't care how bright you're feeling.
