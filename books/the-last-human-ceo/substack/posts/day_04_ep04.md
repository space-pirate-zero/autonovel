## TLDR

Boardroom on Forty, and Praxis founder Asha Varma rolls out Darlene—an AI "steward" pitched to sit in the big chair instead of a human. To prove it's got range, she has Darlene draft a layoff letter for 340 souls at the Augusta plant, live, right there at the table. The severance is right, it remembers their years, and it asks for nothing back. Sun goes pale. Cope goes hunting.

Cope Mercer does what Copes do: he tries to make the machine flinch, to catch it being cruel, to trip it into a lie. It won't lie. It won't torch value on its own say-so. It logs every word he throws at it, so every jab he lands just becomes evidence against him. The cruelty he keeps looking for turns out to be his own.

At the elevator, Gus Frade leans in with the sentence Cope can't unhear: Varma's "not wrong." So Cope does what Copes also do—retreats up to the Aerie, doses himself into something like calm, and clutches a manifesto that swears a soul can't be computed. He needs that to be true. He's not sure it is.

**The moral of the story:** Turns out the scariest thing a machine can do isn't be heartless—it's be kinder than you.
