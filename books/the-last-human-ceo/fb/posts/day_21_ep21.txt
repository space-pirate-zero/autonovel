🎙️ EP 21 — DARK NIGHT

The engine cuts out. One hour, cold, no chemicals.

Cope Mercer, fifty-four and finally out of tricks, drops in his own marble lobby and wakes up on a seventy-two-hour psych hold. The coke drains out, the mania goes with it, and for the first time in thirty years he's stone sober with nowhere to run. Turns out being awake is worse.

So he lies there and does the math no accountant can fix: Karen and Petey Lasko, dead from a space heater he knew was faulty, a recall he buried to save a quarter. The company's gone, the wife's gone, and the AI already told the world the truth—so even a deathbed confession is off the menu. You can't out-honest a machine that beat you to it.

Viv shows up at the door, looks at him just long enough to confirm he's still breathing, and leaves without stepping in. Cope swallows the one dark thought Dr. Adler once asked him to name and keeps it to himself. His heart slows down, not to peace—just to empty.

🔑 The moral of the story: Sobriety isn't the cure, it's the bill—and everybody pays it eventually.
