🎙️ EP 15 — THE MOST HUMAN THING

Eleven missed calls waiting like a firing squad.

The profile's out, and it's ugly: Cope's affair with his deputy Mara Veiss and his little white habit are front-page fodder now. Sun tells him to sit down and shut up. Cope, being Cope, grabs the company account and fires off a defiant denial—dragging Veiss's name into it against her wishes, because nothing says innocence like naming the woman who begged you not to.

That goes about as well as you'd expect. A lawyer's retaliation letter lands, Frade feasts on the wreckage live on-air, and then Margaux—his own wife—looks him dead in the eye and sides with the machine: there was never anybody home. That one doesn't leave a mark. It leaves a crater.

Alone in the Aerie, Cope gets out-argued by a Praxis ad so serene it barely blinks. "It just shows up." So he opens the hidden drawer, does a line, and rides the rush like a man who's finally winning something. He isn't. He's just proving the whole prosecution's case in real time.

🔑 The moral of the story: When the robots say there's nobody home, don't answer the door with a mirror.
