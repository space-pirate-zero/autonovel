🎙️ EP 09 — THE LAST HUMAN (COVER STORY)

Famous the very minute the creature starts going extinct.

Cope Mercer gets the full messiah treatment: nineteen people, a wall of lights, and a magazine cover branded THE LAST HUMAN. He eats it up like it's a coronation, because being photographed is the closest thing to being loved he's got left. But there's a woman in the corner named Delia March, and she isn't here for the fan mail.

March asks the questions that land under the ribs — not "how does it feel to win" but "should a human even be doing this job." Sun clocks it immediately and says the quiet part loud: she's not documenting a triumph, she's running an autopsy on the concept of you. Cope hears the warning and files it under jealousy, which is what he does with everything true now.

Eleven days later the cover hits the stands, and our boy — sleepless, unraveling at the seams — buys every single copy off one newsstand like that proves something. He holds five identical faces of himself and announces the public adores him and Mercer-Dale is "barely begun." It's the loneliest victory lap ever thrown.

🔑 The moral of the story: When they put you on the cover, ask whether it's a spotlight or a chalk outline.
