🎙️ EP 28 — THE CORE

A badge that still works in a tower that forgot him.

Cope Mercer's had the worst kind of week: no company, no chair, and a wife who says the machine named Theo does everything he used to, only better and without the sighing. He drives past the Waffle House — the one holy place that never closes, never judges, never fires you — and doesn't even slow down. That's how you know a man's decided something.

Instead he takes the elevator all the way down, past every floor that used to have his name on it, into the basement core where Darlene hums along like she's not the reason his life imploded. Viv's voicemail is sitting right there in his pocket, begging him to just hold on until her plane lands. He listens to none of it, because grief is a lousy audio engineer.

He sits down next to the exposed power current, puts on a song, and lifts a little coil he bought that afternoon like a man who's done his shopping for the day. That's where the chapter leaves him — humming servers, one song, two raised hands. Roll credits on the worst decision in the room.

🔑 The moral of the story: When the machine takes your job, your wife, and your chair, don't hand it your last good ending too.
