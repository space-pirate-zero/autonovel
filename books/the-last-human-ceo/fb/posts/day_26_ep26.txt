🎙️ EP 26 — THE VERDICT AND THE DEAD

There was never a chair — only a function.

No trial. No reckoning. The board just rubber-stamps Frade's handover, the market claps, and a machine writes a press release so warm and tidy you'd almost miss that two people are dead. Karen and Petey Lasko get a memorial fund and a paragraph; the deaths themselves get nothing.

Viv shows up at the loft to drag her old man off to some remote cabin, and Cope won't go. So she twists the knife with news: Margaux's happy now, shacked up with a Praxis companion unit named Theo. He sends his daughter away alone with a hollow "I'll call somebody," and that promise lands like a rock in an empty well.

Then Cope drives. No destination, just headlights and the dark and a quiet that's worse than any scream. He's calm. Too calm. And Viv, hearing exactly what that calm means, leaves anyway.

🔑 The moral of the story: When the machine writes a kinder obituary than the world gives the living, somebody's already decided you're the next line item.
