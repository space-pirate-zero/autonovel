Prescott "Cope" Mercer, aged fifty-four, collapsed in his building's lobby and woke under an involuntary seventy-two-hour psychiatric hold, hitting a crushing crash as his cocaine-fueled mania drained away and forcing him, in his first fully sober hour in thirty years, to confront the deaths of Karen and Petey Lasko—killed by a faulty Mercer space heater whose recall he'd suppressed to protect a quarter—along with the loss of his company, his wife, and any possibility of a redemptive confession, since the truth had already been exposed by the AI system. His daughter Viv appeared briefly at the door, refusing to come in or offer comfort, and left after confirming it was him, while Cope silently concealed the dark, unnamed thought Dr. Adler had once asked about and lay alone as his racing heart finally slowed toward an ordinary, hollowed-out rhythm.
