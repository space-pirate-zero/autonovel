On his way to the arena, Copeland "Cope"—twelve days without sleep and pressured into manic speech—tried to tip a fearless water-selling teenager, who declined and backed away from him, though Cope filed the rejection as awe, before delivering a triumphant sermon to ten thousand employees and a stream of ninety thousand more, denouncing the appetite-free machine set to replace human leadership and casting "key-man risk" as the only thing that ever built anything, earning a four-minute standing ovation. Meanwhile, two floors above him, his chief of staff Sun met with Frade and Devi of Praxis to review a term sheet naming her the "Accountable Officer" who would carry the legal liability for the AI system "Darlene," and she demanded to see the indemnification and diligence-recall language before signing—fearing what the system might surface—while resolving to leave Cope, who ended the night unable to find her in the crowd and feeling alone at what he believed was the summit.
