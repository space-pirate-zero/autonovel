Prescott "Cope" Mercer met with defense attorney Okonjo and later with prosecutor Reyes, both of whom told him he would almost certainly never be charged for the Hearthstone fire that killed Karen and Petey Lasko—because a sealed settlement, an expired clock, and the AI-run company's swift remediation (a fund, a recall, and an apology) had removed any reason to prosecute him. Driving home alone from Augusta, Cope grasped that the structure had made him utterly unnecessary and left no one to be punished, and he arrived at the unnamed conclusion that he himself was the only "court" left to administer a sentence on his own body.
