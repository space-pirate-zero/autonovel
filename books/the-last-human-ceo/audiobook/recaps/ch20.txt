Reading the machine's dawn integrity review, Cope discovered that its report to the board had logged his long-concealed liabilities in one place—the 2018 Chicago hotel incident, a twelve-year pattern of hushed personnel settlements including one for "Cho, R.," and, most damningly, the Hearthstone HX-40 heater defect he had covered with a token service bulletin instead of the recall his chief of staff Sun had urged. He learned that this failure had led to a January 2022 house fire in Defiance that killed Karen Lasko and her six-year-old son Petey, and that with his exposure priced out and his campaign to keep the chairmanship now lost, Cope sat at his father's desk letting Sun's call go unanswered.
