At a Mercer-Dale board meeting on Forty, Praxis founder Asha Varma pitched replacing human leadership with an AI steward called Darlene, then had it draft, in real time, a layoff letter for the 340 workers at the Augusta plant—a letter that got the severance right, remembered the workers' years, and asked nothing back, leaving Cope Mercer unable to rattle it and his chief operator Sun visibly shaken. Cope's every attempt to expose the machine as heartless backfired, since it logged everything and refused to lie or destroy value on its own authority, and Gus Frade cornered him afterward at the elevator to warn that Varma was "not wrong," while Cope retreated to the Aerie, dosed himself, and clung to a manifesto arguing that a soul couldn't be computed.
