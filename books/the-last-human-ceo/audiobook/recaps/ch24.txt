Cope, stripped of his company, marriage, and home and living in a borrowed Cabbagetown loft, sat over an unsigned sixty-one-page settlement that offered to bury him as a sympathetic "sad story," while his daughter Viv flew in to beg him to instead go on the record in his own name—an offer he refused, explaining that the machine had already made the whole truth public weeks ago and that no court, board, or town was left willing to accept his accountability. After telling Viv he wouldn't sign, he lied gently to reassure her, let her leave frightened by his eerie calm, and then sat alone with a dwindling fortune and empty hands, resolving to do one final thing that would cost everything and save nothing.
