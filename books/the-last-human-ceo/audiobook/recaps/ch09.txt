Cope Mercer sat for a lavish magazine photo shoot titled "THE LAST HUMAN," worked over by a crew of nineteen and studied throughout by Delia March, the profile writer whose penetrating questions unsettled him even as he read the campaign as a triumph, while Sun warned him that March was writing about whether a human should run a company and was studying, not aiding, him. Eleven days later Cope, sleepless and quietly deteriorating, bought all five copies of the finished cover from a newsstand and declared that the public loved him and that Mercer-Dale was "barely begun."
