Cope Mercer spent the week convinced his campaign was winning while ignoring mounting warning signs—journalist Delia March's cold fact-checking calls, his lawyer Hal Brennan's cryptic warnings to avoid the press, rival Frade's needling TV attacks, and his wife Margaux telling him she'd stopped counting his affairs and was now counting something she refused to name before retreating upstairs. After flying out a young staffer named Mara at dawn and spending predawn hours seeking anonymous erasure at the edge of the city, he arrived at his office and, with his phone having gone dark for eleven silent calls during the drive, finally saw the eleven missed calls waiting for him.
