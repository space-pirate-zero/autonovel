Cope was cornered in his own Buckhead living room by his wife Margaux, daughter Viv, and a psychiatrist named Dr. Adler, who bluntly diagnosed him with a manic episode compounded by a long-standing cocaine addiction, refused to let him treat one as an excuse for the other, and—before he charmed his way out without answering whether he was suicidal—pressed her cell number into his pocket and made him promise to call before doing anything drastic. Margaux told him there would be no "after" and that he was going to lose everything, after which Cope drove off, refolded his paper at the foot of the drive, and retreated to a Waffle House where the waitress asked him nothing, while the building's automated system quietly cleared his Monday morning schedule without his request.
