Cope Mercer learned that the Tidewater fund had filed a 13D to replace him with an AURELIUS system, and he watched Gus Frade brand him a "beloved liability" on Halloran's show, resolving to fight back publicly with a campaign he called "The Human Premium." Despite urgent warnings from both his aide Sun and his daughter Viv not to go on air, Cope dismissed their fears, texted a columnist against legal advice, and—relying increasingly on the drug from his desk drawer—kept drafting his counterargument while Sun agreed to cancel the Halloran appearance to stop him.
